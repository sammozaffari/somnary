5-HTP is sold for sleep on a tidy piece of biochemistry: it's the immediate precursor to serotonin,
which the body turns into melatonin. the theory is appealing. the human evidence, and the safety
math, are not.

on evidence: there is essentially no trial of 5-HTP **on its own** for sleep. the one study we
identified tested an amino-acid combination of 5-HTP *and* GABA in 18 people, and while that mixture
shortened the time to fall asleep, it cannot tell you what 5-HTP contributes by itself
<CiteMarker n={1} />. that's a D: the claims run far ahead of the standalone data.

on safety, 5-HTP is the rare D where the safety section outweighs the efficacy one. because it
directly raises serotonin, combining it with common serotonergic medicines (SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, or
migraine triptans) can cause **serotonin syndrome**, which can be life-threatening. treat that as a
hard rule: don't stack 5-HTP with those drugs without medical supervision. and there's a cautionary
history nearby: L-tryptophan, a closely related precursor, was pulled from the market in 1989 after a
contaminant caused a serious illness called eosinophilia-myalgia syndrome <CiteMarker n={2} />. that's a
reminder that a "natural" precursor is only as safe as its purity and what you take it with.
