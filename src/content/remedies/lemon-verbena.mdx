the first thing to get right about lemon verbena is that it isn't lemon balm. they're both lemony,
calming kitchen herbs, and the names get swapped constantly — but lemon verbena is *Aloysia citriodora*
(older names *Lippia citriodora*, *Aloysia triphylla*), while lemon balm is *Melissa officinalis*, a
separate plant with its own page and its own evidence. different family, different active compound
(verbascoside here, rosmarinic acid there). we grade them separately, and this page is only about lemon
verbena.

the sleep case is better than folklore. two small placebo-controlled RCTs of a standardized lemon
verbena extract improved sleep-quality scores: a 40-person trial in stressed poor sleepers (400 mg/day
for eight weeks) improved PSQI and lowered cortisol <CiteMarker n={2} />, and a 71-person trial over 90
days improved PSQI, sleep latency and efficiency, *and* two more objective measures — actigraphy and
nocturnal melatonin <CiteMarker n={1} />. objective-ish signals like those set it apart from most
fragrant sleep teas, which have nothing of the kind.

but read who ran the trials. every human sleep study of lemon verbena tests the same proprietary
extract (RelaxPLX) from the same manufacturer, with the manufacturer's staff among the authors; a third
trial, in adolescents, is the same story <CiteMarker n={3} />. none has been independently replicated,
none enrolled people with diagnosed insomnia (the populations were healthy adults with poor sleep or
moderate stress), and the sleep improvement tracked a fall in stress and cortisol — so, as with lemon
balm, the better sleep may ride on the calm. the receptor mechanism (GABA-A and adenosine A1, via
verbascoside) is real but so far shown only in mice <CiteMarker n={4} />.

that mix is why it lands at **D**. the placebo-controlled RCT with objective actigraphy and melatonin
data is what kept it in the conversation for a C — but manufacturer-only, single-product, unreplicated
evidence is exactly the pattern this site grades *down* on a coin-flip, and with no independent
replication it sits at D. if your poor sleep is stress-driven, lemon verbena has a modest, real, early
case — but it is not a treatment for insomnia, and the concentrated extract used in the trials is not
the same thing as a cup of verbena tea.
