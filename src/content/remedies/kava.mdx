kava is the remedy that proves an F on this site does not mean "it doesn't work."

it works — for anxiety. a cochrane meta-analysis of eleven randomized trials found kava extract
was an effective symptomatic treatment for anxiety compared with placebo, with mild and transient
side effects in the trials themselves <CiteMarker n={1} />. that is a real result, and we're not
going to pretend otherwise.

the problem is the liver. kava is a documented cause of serious hepatotoxicity: a CDC surveillance
report counted **eleven patients who developed liver failure and needed a transplant** across the
United States and Europe, and the FDA issued a consumer advisory in 2002 <CiteMarker n={3} />. a
detailed clinical review judged kava potentially hepatotoxic across every extract type (water,
ethanol, and acetone), with risk rising from high doses, prolonged use, combining it with other
drugs, and poor-quality raw material <CiteMarker n={2} />.

and note what's missing: none of the good evidence is about **sleep**. kava's sleep reputation is
borrowed from its calming, sedative effect on anxiety. it has never been shown to treat insomnia in
a trial. so you'd be taking on a documented risk of liver failure for a benefit that was never
demonstrated. that is the definition of an **F**: the risk outweighs the evidence.
