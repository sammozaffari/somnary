# Audience, Personas, Needstates, And Audience Pages

## Audience Architecture

Somnary should not serve “everyone who sleeps.” That is too broad to guide content, product, or growth decisions. The most valuable audience is people making a supplement or remedy decision under uncertainty.

## Primary Persona: The Evidence-Seeking Sleep Improver

Profile:

- adult, health-literate, but not a clinician;
- considering melatonin, magnesium, CBD, valerian, L-theanine, ashwagandha, or a sleep blend;
- has read conflicting advice;
- wants a concrete next step without being sold a product;
- may be anxious because sleep affects work, parenting, mood, or health.

Core need:

- “Tell me whether this is worth trying, what the real evidence says, what the risks are, and when I should stop self-experimenting.”

Jobs to be done:

- compare remedies by goal;
- understand effect size in plain English;
- spot unsafe or dishonest labels;
- know what questions to ask a clinician;
- avoid wasting money on weak products;
- avoid taking something inappropriate with medications, pregnancy, children, liver risk, or next-day safety concerns.

## Secondary Persona 1: The Cautious Caregiver

Need:

- pediatric melatonin clarity, accidental ingestion risk, and “talk to a pediatrician” boundaries.

Recommended audience page:

- title: “Before giving a child melatonin”
- top answer: “Treat melatonin as a short-term clinical conversation, not a nightly vitamin.”
- modules: what melatonin is for, what it is not for, gummy-label accuracy, overdose risk, autism/ADHD nuance, questions for pediatrician, storage safety.

Gap analysis:

- Somnary has context around melatonin in children, but the homepage does not route worried parents quickly enough.

Opportunity:

- parent-focused pages can earn search, backlinks, and newsletter subscribers because the risk is urgent and shareable.

## Secondary Persona 2: The Older Adult Or Medication User

Need:

- interaction and fall-risk awareness before trying sedating products.

Recommended audience page:

- title: “Before using a sleep supplement with medications”
- modules: blood thinners, sedatives, antidepressants, antihistamines, CBD, magnesium, kidney disease, liver-risk botanicals, pharmacist checklist.

Gap analysis:

- remedy pages have safety sections, but a medication user needs one cross-remedy safety hub.

Opportunity:

- build pharmacist-friendly handouts and referral credibility.

## Secondary Persona 3: The Anxious Poor Sleeper

Need:

- distinguish anxiety-calming evidence from true insomnia evidence.

Recommended audience page:

- title: “When anxiety is what keeps you awake”
- modules: what counts as anxiety-driven insomnia, CBT-I vs calming supplements, lavender/Silexan nuance, L-theanine, ashwagandha cautions, when anxiety needs care.

Gap analysis:

- Somnary has outcome pages, but they can feel like ranked lists rather than a pathway.

Opportunity:

- this page can prevent overclaiming and make the site feel more empathetic.

## Secondary Persona 4: Shift Worker Or Traveler

Need:

- circadian timing clarity rather than sedation.

Recommended audience page:

- title: “Jet lag and shift work: what helps the body clock”
- modules: melatonin timing, light timing, caffeine timing, when not to use sedatives, safety-critical work, realistic expectations.

Gap analysis:

- melatonin is correctly described as a body-clock tool, but the site should make the circadian use case a first-class route.

Opportunity:

- strong YouTube and SEO hook: “Melatonin is a clock signal, not a sleeping pill.”

## Secondary Persona 5: Clinician, Pharmacist, Or Health Writer

Need:

- fast source-backed summaries, patient handouts, and no affiliate contamination.

Recommended audience page:

- title: “Evidence summaries for clinicians and pharmacists”
- modules: grade rubric, source list, printable patient handouts, correction policy, citation export.

Gap analysis:

- methodology is strong but not packaged as professional tooling.

Opportunity:

- professional backlinks and credibility moat.

## Audience Page Template

Every audience page should include:

1. The plain-English bottom line.
2. What this page can help with.
3. What this page cannot do.
4. The relevant remedies by evidence grade.
5. Safety boundaries first.
6. Label traps for this audience.
7. What to ask a clinician or pharmacist.
8. Related Somnary pages.
9. Sources and review date.
