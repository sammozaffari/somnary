## Gate evidence (why excluded)

**Active ingredients per 9 g scoop / 205 mL prepared dose** (verbatim, brand page + Chemist Warehouse, 2026-07-22):
- Magnesium (from **Magnesium glycinate**) 300 mg
- Magnesium (from **Magnesium citrate**) 120 mg
- **Total elemental Magnesium 420 mg**
- **Glycine 1.9 g** (1,900 mg) — *free glycine, distinct from the magnesium-glycinate salt above*
- Humulus lupulus (Hops) extract 100 mg dry concentrate (from 500 mg dry flower)
- Eschscholzia californica (California Poppy) extract 125 mg dry concentrate
- Matricaria chamomilla (Chamomile) extract 55.5 mg dry concentrate (from 500 mg dry flower)

**Lead-ingredient test:** FAILS. The magnesium salt (glycinate + citrate) and the three herbal
actives frame the product ("Magnesium which supports nervous system function"; "Hops … to induce
sleep"). Glycine at 1.9 g is a co-active, not the lead, and does not reach the studied ~3 g in one
serving.

**Dose-honesty:** 1.9 g free glycine per serving = ~63% of the studied 3 g. Underdosed for glycine.

**Additives note (for the record):** contains **sucralose** and **sulfites** as declared allergens
(Chemist Warehouse, 2026-07-22) — so NOT a pure glycine powder. Brand copy says "no artificial
flavours or sweeteners," which sits in tension with the sucralose allergen declaration; noted, not
adjudicated (this product is excluded regardless).

**Regulatory / grade:** No AUST L number displayed on the pages read (2026-07-22). No
"pharmaceutical grade," purity, or third-party testing claim stated. Warning: "Do not use if
pregnant or likely to become pregnant."
