# Glycine candidate product universe (AU-first) — `[HUMAN-GATE]`

**Status:** candidate list for owner review; human-gated so selection can't be accused of
cherry-picking. Sourced by a research agent (2026-07-20), trimmed to a 12-product pilot. No product
here is scored, ranked, or judged — this is only *which products we research*.

## Inclusion criteria, applied evenly

- **Glycine (the amino acid, taken for sleep) is the lead ingredient.** Single-ingredient glycine
  prioritised; a bounded set of common AU glycine-forward sleep combos admitted, each labeled `combo`.
  The sleep dose in the trials (Kawai/Inagawa) is **3 g before bed** — that is the reference point.
- **PURITY + DOSE HONESTY is the quality axis** (glycine has no "form" — it is a single amino acid, so
  the valerian/magnesium "standardisation/form" axis is replaced by): is the powder pharmaceutical/USP
  grade vs generic? Is there **independent third-party testing (USP/NSF/Informed) or a public COA**?
  Is the excipient panel **clean and fully disclosed** (pure glycine vs anti-caking/flow agents/
  sweeteners)? And **does a label serving actually deliver ~3 g**, or is it an underdosed sprinkle /
  a small capsule that forces 6 caps to reach the studied dose? Non-disclosure is recorded, not skipped.
- **The FOOD vs AUST L medicine distinction is captured for every product.** Most bulk/pure glycine
  powders are sold as **FOODS / supplements with NO AUST L number** (no TGA medicine oversight of the
  label, claims, or quality); a minority — the therapeutic-claim sleep combos — are **TGA-listed
  medicines with an AUST L**. This split is a first-class signal here, not a footnote.
- **Availability:** Chemist Warehouse / Amazon AU / online AU chemists (AU brands) + iHerb AU (imports).
  AUST L captured where the product is a listed medicine; imports carry none (personal-importation).
- **Quality spectrum evenly:** mainstream AU chemist, practitioner-tier AU, bulk-powder AU, and an
  iHerb-imported contrast — reached deliberately so the set is not stacked toward "clean" products.

## Deliberate exclusions

- **Magnesium glycinate / bisglycinate products (Swisse Magnesium Glycinate, Herbs of Gold, Nutra-Life
  Deep Sleep + Magnesium Glycinate, most "sleep + magnesium" tablets)** — the magnesium salt of glycine
  is NOT glycine dosed for sleep; it is a magnesium product. Excluded from the glycine universe on the
  lead-ingredient test, though the distinction is called out in the notes because it is the single most
  common confusion for this remedy.
- **BioCeuticals Ultra Muscleze Night** — glycine is present but only **250 mg** per serving (a trace),
  buried in a magnesium/inositol/choline combo; fails the lead-ingredient / meaningful-dose test.
- **Doctor's Best, Jarrow** — no standalone pure-glycine product exists (Doctor's Best "glycine" hits are
  all magnesium lysinate glycinate chelate). Not fabricated into cards.
- **Nature's Own Complete Sleep, Herbs of Gold Sleep Ease, Ethical Nutrients Triple Action Sleep,
  Caruso's Sleep More** — herbal/Lactium sleep formulas that contain **NO glycine**; brand has sleep
  positioning but fails the lead-ingredient test.
- **Pure Encapsulations Glycine, Life Extension Glycine, Source Naturals Glycine, Double Wood Glycine,
  GO Healthy Good Night, Designs for Health Glycine Powder, Pure Wellness / Pure Product AU powders** —
  all real single-glycine (or glycine-forward) products, held out only to keep the pilot at 12 without
  over-weighting the imported-capsule segment (Pure Encaps/Life Extension/Source Naturals/Solgar all
  occupy the same "500 mg–1 g cap, no COA" niche as candidates already in the set) or the AU food-grade
  powder segment (HealthWise/Bulk Nutrients/VPA already represent it). Designs for Health held because
  **AU reachability could not be confirmed** this pass (all confirmed retailers were US). Any of these
  can swap in without re-running the search.

## Notes for the owner

- **The purity/testing axis (glycine's analogue of standardisation):** because glycine is a single amino
  acid, the sourcing question is not "which form" but **who verified the purity and dose**. The pilot is
  built to contrast that: NOW ("Pharmaceutical Grade (USP)") and Swanson AjiPure (Ajinomoto's branded
  pharmaceutical-grade raw material) make the **strongest explicit grade claims**; Bulk Nutrients (AU)
  and HealthWise claim **pharmaceutical grade** as foods; the imported capsules (Solgar, Life Extension,
  Source Naturals) make **no grade claim** at all. This is the cleanest transparency contrast in the set.
- **Third-party testing — thin across the board, and worth flagging at scoring:** only **Thorne**
  ("third-party certified") and **BulkSupplements.com** ("third-party lab tests each batch") make an
  explicit third-party-testing claim; **Bulk Nutrients (AU)** publishes batch COAs. But **no brand in the
  set offers a verifiable public per-lot COA *lookup* for glycine** — NOW's CoFA portal is essential-oil
  only, and every other "third-party tested" line is marketing text, not a document a reader can open.
  **No glycine product in the pilot holds USP Verified / NSF / Informed certification.** Treat all
  "third-party tested" language as a claim to verify, not a certification, at scoring.
- **Food vs AUST L medicine split (capture per-product):** the **pure glycine powders are FOODS with no
  AUST L** (HealthWise states verbatim "sold as a food only"; Bulk Nutrients, VPA, and all iHerb imports
  likewise carry no AUST L). The **therapeutic-claim sleep combos are TGA-listed medicines** —
  Blackmores Sleep Sound (**AUST L 312974**, to re-confirm on the ARTG) and Ethical Nutrients Mega
  Magnesium Night (AUST L present; number to confirm). So the single-ingredient glycine a consumer takes
  for sleep sits **outside** TGA medicine oversight, while the magnesium-combo does not — a structural
  point the scorecard should make legible.
- **Underdosing red flags (dose honesty):** only the **powders deliver ~3 g in one serving** — Blackmores
  (3.1 g), HealthWise (~3.5 g), Bulk Nutrients (3 g), VPA (~3 g), NOW powder (3 g), BulkSupplements
  (3 g). The **capsules force multi-cap loads to reach the studied dose**: **Solgar and Thorne need 6
  caps** for 3 g; NOW caps and Life Extension need 3. Practitioner/combo products are underdosed at label
  serving: **Orthoplex 1.5 g**, **Ethical Nutrients Mega Mag Night 0.74 g free glycine**, and (excluded)
  BioCeuticals 0.25 g. Whether "would take 6 capsules to reach the studied dose" is a sourcing signal
  (dose honesty) or an efficacy/evidence matter is a `[HUMAN-GATE]` rubric question to settle at scoring
  — recommend it reads as *dose honesty / additives-&-formulation* context, mirroring the magnesium
  oxide ruling (potency of the delivered dose informs the read, but the amino acid itself is not penalised).
- **Capture gaps to close before scoring:** AUST L numbers for Blackmores (312974) and Ethical Nutrients
  need ARTG confirmation on a first-party page; several excipient panels (Thorne, Solgar, NOW caps) were
  read from retailer snippets and should be confirmed against a physical label; Bulk Nutrients / VPA exact
  per-serving glycine and COA availability should be confirmed on a live brand page; and community-read
  themes must be gathered from public forums by a human (vendor star-reviews are excluded by README rule).
