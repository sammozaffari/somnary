# Glycine dossiers — consolidation & scoring inputs (AU pilot)

Research complete for all 12 candidates (2026-07-20). **Gather-only: no scores drafted here.** Read
before the scoring pass. Owner greenlit the 12-product universe and ruled dose-honesty = formulation
CONTEXT (a plain note, not a scored penalty), mirroring the magnesium-oxide ruling.

## The central glycine story: there is no "form" — the axis is PURITY + DOSE HONESTY + who verified it

Glycine is a single amino acid, so the valerian/magnesium standardisation-vs-form axis doesn't apply.
What separates glycine products is (1) has anyone independently verified purity/content, (2) does a
label serving actually deliver the studied ~3 g, and (3) is the glycine a TGA-listed medicine or an
unregulated food. All three split the set cleanly.

## Testing & certification — the flat, honest floor of this category
- **No product in the set holds a finished-product third-party certification** (USP Verified / NSF /
  Informed). Every "third-party tested / pharmaceutical grade" line is a claim, not a certifier mark.
- **Thorne is the key correction vs magnesium:** Thorne's *magnesium* was directory-confirmed NSF
  Certified for Sport (scored testing/label 2 on the magnesium page). Its **glycine SKU is NOT in the
  NSF directory** (checked 2026-07-20) — 35+ Thorne products are listed, glycine is not. So Thorne
  glycine's "third-party certified" line has no directory backing; it's facility-level quality only.
- **BulkSupplements** — the one hard, directory-verifiable signal in the set: NSF's GMP registered-
  FACILITY directory lists its legal entity (Hard Eight Nutrition LLC, Henderson NV). But that is a
  facility GMP registration, NOT a product certification, and its "third-party lab tests each batch"
  resolves to **COA-on-request** (a support-ticket form), not a public per-lot lookup.
- **Bulk Nutrients (AU)** runs a genuine PUBLIC COA program ("Lab Results" tab per product) — the
  strongest COA model here — BUT a glycine-specific COA PDF could not be confirmed posted (the tab is
  JS-rendered; featured COAs are Creatine/Protein/etc). Treat as plausible-but-unconfirmed; verify a
  live glycine COA before crediting a per-lot document.
- **NOW** declines to produce supplement COAs for consumers (CoFA portal is essential-oil-scoped) —
  its loud grade claim is paired with no openable document.

## Grade claims — a real transparency contrast (all self-asserted, one has a pedigree)
- **NOW Pure Powder** — the loudest: **"Pharmaceutical Grade (USP)"** — but "(USP)" is a spec/grade
  claim, NOT a USP Verified certification (not in USP's directory). NOW caps drop even the "(USP)".
- **Swanson AjiPure** — the most substantive: glycine is Ajinomoto's branded **AjiPure** pharma-grade
  amino acid (named, reputable raw-material maker; fermentation-based; "USP/EP compliant"). A real
  RAW-MATERIAL pedigree — stronger than a bare self-claim — but still not a finished-product cert.
- **HealthWise, Bulk Nutrients, VPA** — "100% pure pharmaceutical grade," self-asserted, on FOODS.
- **Solgar** — makes **no grade claim at all** (a clean contrast); relies on brand reputation.

## Dose honesty (framing CONTEXT per owner ruling, not scored)
- **Meets the studied ~3 g in ONE serving:** HealthWise (3.5 g/tsp), NOW Pure Powder (3 g/¾ tsp),
  BulkSupplements (3 g), Bulk Nutrients (~3–4 g, label basis to confirm), Blackmores combo (3.1 g).
- **Multi-capsule load to reach 3 g:** NOW caps = 3 caps; **Thorne = 6 caps; Solgar = 6 caps** (the
  heaviest). Swanson powder ~3 scoops.
- **Underdosed at label serving:** Orthoplex 1.5 g (half); **Ethical Nutrients Night 0.74 g free
  glycine** (a quarter — glycine is a minor co-active in a magnesium/passionflower combo).
- Per owner ruling: note these plainly in the verdict/formulation read; do NOT deduct a score for them.

## Food vs AUST L medicine (a first-class structural signal)
- **The pure glycine people take for sleep is a FOOD, outside TGA medicine oversight** — HealthWise,
  Bulk Nutrients, VPA, Orthoplex, and all iHerb imports (NOW, Thorne, Swanson, Solgar, BulkSupplements)
  carry NO AUST L. HealthWise states "sold as a food only" verbatim; Orthoplex's sponsor Bio Concepts
  is a prolific TGA medicine sponsor yet chose to sell this SKU as a food.
- **Only the therapeutic-claim combos are TGA-listed medicines:** Blackmores Sleep Sound (AUST L
  312974), Ethical Nutrients Mega Magnesium Night (AUST L 490772). So the products with a TGA listing
  are exactly the ones where glycine is a minor, underdosed co-active — a genuine tension worth surfacing.

## Regulatory record — all benign / off-product (held, not deducted)
No primary regulator doc names any glycine product here for a glycine matter. Off-product brand history
(HELD): NOW (2004 ginseng warning letter, NOW Real Food Salmonella recalls); BulkSupplements/Hard Eight
(2015 caffeine WL, 2025 inositol recall, 2018 shellfish recall, mag-glycinate mislabeling class actions);
Thorne (2014 Captomer recall, 2004 warning letter); Solgar (2001 Digestive Aid salmonella recall);
Ethical Nutrients (B6 recall = the Raspberry SKU, batch 007764 — this Night SKU has no B6); Orthoplex
(2025 iodine recall of OTHER Orthoplex SKUs); Bulk Nutrients/Bioflex (off-product cancellation + recall).

## Additives — mostly clean, a category strength
Single-ingredient powders (HealthWise, NOW powder, VPA, Bulk Nutrients, BulkSupplements, Swanson,
Orthoplex "Excipients Nil") are genuinely pure — a real contrast to the AU combos. Capsule imports
(NOW caps, Thorne, Solgar) disclose benign shells (hypromellose, silica, magnesium stearate — all
watchlist `not_flagged`). The two AU combos (Blackmores, Ethical Nutrients) don't publish full panels
online → additives provisional 5 for those, firm 5 for the disclosed-panel singles.
