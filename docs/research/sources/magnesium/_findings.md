# Magnesium dossiers — consolidation & owner decisions (AU pilot)

Research complete for all 12 candidates (2026-07-19/20). **Gather-only: no scores drafted.**

## The central AU story: vitamin B6

The single biggest AU-specific finding. The TGA is actively enforcing against **vitamin B6 in
magnesium supplements** over peripheral-neuropathy risk (250+ adverse-event reports to 31 Oct
2025; mandated on-pack neuropathy warnings; B6-listed-medicine cancellations underway). This is
the thing most Australians buying "magnesium for sleep" don't know. Per-product B6 status is the
sharpest safety/regulatory differentiator in the set:

- **Contains B6 (in the risk zone):** Ethical Nutrients Mega Magnesium — pyridoxine 50 mg/dose =
  **100 mg/day at label use, the TGA adult maximum**, on-pack neuropathy warning; and a **real
  TGA recall exists in this exact powder family** (Mega Magnesium Raspberry, batch 007764, for
  pyridoxine at ~2× label). BioCeuticals' *regular* Ultra Muscleze + P5P contain B6 (the *Night*
  SKU we researched does not).
- **B6-free (clean on this axis):** Blackmores Sleep Sound, Swisse (both citrate + glycinate
  SKUs), Nature's Own, Caruso's, Bioglan PM, Herbs of Gold Forte, BioCeuticals Muscleze **Night**,
  Cabot, Doctor's Best, Thorne. (Note several brands sell a *sibling* B6 SKU — scope carefully.)

## Per-form disclosure (the transparency differentiator)

Whether a multi-salt product discloses the elemental contribution of EACH magnesium form, or
hides it in a combined total:

- **Fully disclosed:** Blackmores Sleep Sound (citrate 296 + glycinate 24), Herbs of Gold Forte
  (4 salts), Caruso's Super (6 salts), Bioglan PM (3 salts), Cabot (4 salts), Doctor's Best
  (single, elemental + compound).
- **Partly / not disclosed:** BioCeuticals Muscleze Night — manufacturer calls its own UltraMag a
  **"proprietary blend"**, per-form split withheld; Swisse Glycinate discloses elemental only, not
  compound; Thorne discloses elemental only. **Excipient panels are undisclosed online for most AU
  products** (Blackmores, Caruso's, Nature's Own, Bioglan, Herbs of Gold, BioCeuticals) — a
  recurring transparency gap; only allergen lines are published.

## Regulatory record (primary-sourced negatives to weigh)

- **Caruso's** — TGA compliance report (6 Sep 2024) found performance/endurance claims
  unsubstantiated; part of **5 infringement notices totalling $82,500** across three Caruso's
  medicines (13 Mar 2024) + a 2021–22 s14 consent to supply while non-compliant. **Scope check
  before scoring: confirm Super Magnesium is one of the three named medicines.**
- **Ethical Nutrients** — TGA recall in the Mega Magnesium powder family (B6 overage, above).
- **Blackmores** — 2025 B6 class action + 2024 "Daily Magnesium" cancellation name OTHER products,
  NOT Sleep Sound (no B6). Brand context, not a finding against this product.
- **Nature's Own** — June 2026 glass-fragment recall covers OTHER SKUs, not this one.
- Clean (no located action against the product): Swisse ×2, Bioglan, Herbs of Gold, Cabot,
  BioCeuticals Night, Doctor's Best, Thorne.

## Certification / testing (who's actually third-party verified)

- **Thorne Magnesium Bisglycinate — NSF Certified for Sport CONFIRMED for this exact SKU** in
  NSF's own directory (id 1205063). The ONLY product in the set with a genuine, directory-verified
  third-party product certification. (Contrast the melatonin Thorne Melaton-5 badge mismatch.)
- Everyone else: no USP/NSF/Informed product cert. AU brands rely on **AUST L (TGA listing)** —
  confirmed for Swisse citrate (355159), Swisse glycinate (460629), Blackmores (312974), Caruso's
  (298635), Bioglan (353356), Herbs of Gold (465031), Cabot (221456), BioCeuticals Night (366872),
  Ethical Nutrients powder (489275). **Imports carry no AUST L** (Doctor's Best, Thorne) — a real
  AU signal (no TGA oversight of that specific product).

- Most others: no flagged additive found, BUT excipient panels are largely undisclosed online, so

## Two rubric questions to settle BEFORE scoring (owner)

1. **Magnesium oxide.** Poorly bioavailable, but present it as a *form/efficacy* fact (remedy-grade
   territory) or does an oxide-heavy blend affect a *sourcing* score? Affects Bioglan (oxide-heavy),
   Caruso's (~85% oxide+phosphate). Recommend: form is an evidence/efficacy matter, NOT a sourcing
   penalty — but a "high-absorption/bioavailable" MARKETING claim while oxide-heavy IS a marketing-
   honesty issue (Bioglan's "3 bioavailable forms" with 44% oxide).
2. **Hidden per-form breakdown.** Is it an Additives "proprietary-blend"-style deduction, or a
   Transparency-dimension matter? Recommend: Transparency (it's a disclosure question), reserving
   the Additives proprietary-blend deduction for when the ACTIVE dose itself is hidden. Affects
   BioCeuticals ("proprietary blend"), Swisse glycinate, Thorne.
