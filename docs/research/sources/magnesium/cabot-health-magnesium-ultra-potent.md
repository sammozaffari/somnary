## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salts:** four different magnesium forms, with the manufacturer explicitly
  stating oxide is excluded. Full per-form breakdown, quoted verbatim from the
  manufacturer label panel (shop.cabothealth.com.au, read 2026-07-20):
  > "Each level metric teaspoon (5g dose) contains:
  > Taurine 1000 mg
  > Magnesium amino acid chelate (providing elemental magnesium 350 mg) 1750 mg
  > Magnesium ascorbate (providing elemental magnesium 13 mg) 210 mg
  > Magnesium glycinate dihydrate (providing elemental magnesium 12.2 mg) 104 mg
  > Magnesium phosphate pentahydrate (providing elemental magnesium 24.8 mg) 120 mg
  > Total elemental magnesium 400 mg
  > Zinc (as amino acid chelate) 5 mg"
- **Per-form elemental breakdown DISCLOSED: YES — fully.** This is the strongest
  transparency signal in the dossier. Each of the four salts declares BOTH its
  compound weight AND the elemental magnesium it provides, and the four elemental
  figures (350 + 13 + 12.2 + 24.8) sum exactly to the declared **400 mg** total.
  No hidden-blend defect on the magnesium actives.
- **The 400 mg is overwhelmingly from ONE salt.** "Magnesium amino acid chelate"
  provides **350 mg of the 400 mg** (87.5%); the other three salts contribute
  only 50 mg combined (ascorbate 13, glycinate 12.2, phosphate 24.8). So while
  marketed as "4 different forms of magnesium", by elemental mass this is
  essentially a magnesium-amino-acid-chelate product with three minor forms.
  (Gathering, not scoring — a human should weigh whether "4 forms" framing
  reflects the mass distribution.)
- **Chelate identity NOT specified.** The dominant salt is labelled only
  "magnesium amino acid chelate" — the amino acid is **not named** (unlike the
  separately-listed "magnesium glycinate dihydrate"). Cabot does not state
  whether the 1750 mg chelate is a glycinate/bisglycinate, aspartate, or other
  amino-acid chelate. A minor transparency gap: the 87.5%-of-dose form is the
  least specified. (No claim of glycinate is made for it on the label read
  2026-07-20.)
- **Elemental vs compound weight:** both stated for every salt. Total 5 g powder
  → 400 mg elemental.
- **Combo actives (taurine, zinc) individually quantified:** yes (taurine
  1000 mg; zinc 5 mg). No proprietary blend of actives.

- The dominant AU regulatory theme for magnesium supplements is **vitamin B6
  peripheral neuropathy** — magnesium products commonly co-formulate B6, and the
  TGA has escalated action. Primary pages:
  - "Health supplements containing vitamin B6 can cause peripheral neuropathy":
    https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/health-supplements-containing-vitamin-b6-can-cause-peripheral-neuropathy
  - "Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine)":
    https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/vitamin-b6-pyridoxine
  - "Medicines containing vitamin B6 (pyridoxine, pyridoxal or pyridoxamine)":
    https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/medicines-containing-vitamin-b6-pyridoxine-pyridoxal-or-pyridoxamine
  - TGA facts surfaced via search (2026-07-20): as of **31 October 2025** there
    were **"250 reports of peripheral neuropathy"** for vitamin-B6 products; the

## Marketing claims (→ marketing honesty)

Quoted **verbatim** (source + date). Characterization is deliberately avoided.
Source: manufacturer product page
https://shop.cabothealth.com.au/magnesium-ultra-potent-powder-citrus-465g
(read 2026-07-20) unless noted.

**The "NO magnesium oxide" claim (the product's headline quality signal) —
verbatim:**
> "What makes our magnesium so unique and superior to other brands? Each teaspoon
> provides a massive 400mg of elemental magnesium. This provides you with the
> daily therapeutic dose of magnesium in only one teaspoon. We have combined 4
> different forms of magnesium in this powder. **We have excluded magnesium oxide
> from our formula as it is poorly absorbed and can cause a laxative effect.**"

- **Is the "no oxide" claim ACCURATE? YES — confirmed.** Neither the label panel
  (read 2026-07-20) nor the **ARTG ingredient list** (AUST L 221456, read live
  2026-07-20) contains magnesium oxide. The four salts are amino acid chelate,
  ascorbate, glycinate dihydrate, phosphate pentahydrate. The "no oxide" claim is
  a truthful description of the formulation, not just marketing.
- **Absorption framing:** the "poorly absorbed" / "laxative effect" description
  of oxide is the standard bioavailability argument (oxide is genuinely
  low-bioavailability); the page does **not** attach a quantified "Nx better
  absorbed" number or cite a study for the finished product. It attacks the
  **oxide form** on absorption/tolerability, not "fillers."

**"Free from calcium" claim — verbatim:**
> "It is free from calcium. Magnesium helps the muscles relax, whilst calcium has
> the opposite effect (contraction). This is not what you want when you're trying
> to relax!"

**"Free from ... sweeteners" claim (note the stevia tension) — verbatim:**
> "Free from any artificial colours or sweeteners, dairy, yeast, gluten or sugar."
- The claim is specifically **"artificial ... sweeteners"**; the product contains
  **stevia** (a natural, non-artificial sweetener), so the qualifier "artificial"
  makes the statement literally defensible. Captured for the human scorer;
  no characterization applied. (Chemist Warehouse phrases it "no ... artificial
  colours, flavours or preservatives" — 2026-07-20.)

**Health-benefit claims (verbatim):**
> "Reduce stress: Magnesium helps to relax and calm the nervous system. Stress
> depletes magnesium stores in the body..."
> "Headache management: Several studies have reported that magnesium
> supplementation can help to relieve headaches."
> "Decreased muscle cramps: ... magnesium is the most commonly deficient and is
> associated with muscular cramps, spasms and tightness. Many people experience
> relief from their cramps when supplementing with magnesium."
> "Improved sleep: As magnesium helps to calm the nervous system, it is a great
> supplement to take before bed to help reduce stress and evoke relaxation..."
- Chemist Warehouse benefit list (verbatim, 2026-07-20): "Relieve headache
  symptoms / Reduce symptoms of stress / Reduce sleeplessness / Relieve muscle
  cramps." The 465 g product tagline on the brand site: **"Relieves symptoms of
  stress."**

**Observations for the human scorer (gathering, not scoring):** the sleep claim
is framed **indirectly** — sleep benefit is routed through stress reduction
("helping to reduce stress could be beneficial for a restful sleep") rather than
a direct "magnesium makes you sleep" claim. "Several studies have reported"
(headaches) is an unquantified evidence gesture with no citation on-page. The
"unique and superior to other brands" and "massive 400mg" wording is
promotional. No study is cited for the finished product.
