## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific) — PER-FORM BREAKDOWN DISCLOSED

**This product discloses the per-form elemental magnesium breakdown** — a
transparency high-water mark for a multi-salt blend. Captured verbatim from the
manufacturer page (https://www.blackmores.com.au/products/sleep-sound-magnesium)
and corroborated on the Pharmacy Online and Chemist Warehouse listings,
2026-07-19. Active ingredients **per 7.5 g dose:**

- **Magnesium citrate — 2000 mg (2 g)**, providing **magnesium 296 mg**
- **Magnesium glycinate dihydrate — 216 mg**, providing **magnesium 24 mg**
- **Total magnesium — 320 mg** (elemental; the sum is stated on-label, and each
  salt's elemental contribution is itemised separately — NOT a single combined
  total. This is the hidden-blend defect's opposite.)
- **Glycine — 3.1 g (3100 mg)** *(sleep-positioning active, high dose)*
- ***Humulus lupulus* (hops) extract dry concentrate — 125 mg**, equivalent to
  **937.5 mg dry flower** *(sleep-positioning herb; extract-to-herb ratio
  disclosed)*
- **Calcium citrate tetrahydrate — 634 mg**, providing **calcium 133.2 mg**

**Transparency reading (recorded, not scored):**
- **Compound vs elemental weight is stated for BOTH salts** (2000 mg citrate →
  296 mg Mg; 216 mg glycinate → 24 mg Mg). Elemental total (320 mg) is explicit.
- **Per-form elemental split disclosed** (296 mg citrate-derived + 24 mg
  glycinate-derived). The glycinate contributes only ~7.5% of the elemental
  magnesium — a fact only visible BECAUSE the per-form split is disclosed; a
  product declaring only "320 mg magnesium (citrate & glycinate)" would hide it.
- **Herb standardisation disclosed:** hops given as extract (125 mg) with the
  dry-flower equivalent (937.5 mg), i.e. a ~7.5:1 concentrate. Glycine dose
  (3.1 g) stated numerically.
- No proprietary-blend combined weight is used for the actives; each active is
  individually quantified. (Excipients are a separate transparency gap — see
  Additives: the full excipient panel is not published online.)

### CRITICAL DISTINCTION — the Blackmores B6 saga does NOT name THIS product
There is a live 2025 Australian vitamin-B6 controversy and a proposed class
action against Blackmores over **peripheral neuropathy** from high-dose vitamin
B6. It is important to record that it names **other** Blackmores magnesium
products, **not** Sleep Sound Magnesium:

- **Products named in the proposed class action:** **Blackmores Super Magnesium+**
  and **Blackmores Ashwagandha+**. Per Polaris Lawyers, "Blackmores Super
  Magnesium+ contained approximately 29 times the Recommended Daily Intake (RDI)
  of vitamin B6." Lead plaintiff **Dominic Noonan-O'Keeffe**; proposed class
  action announced **May 2025**.
  Source: https://www.polarislawyers.com.au/vitaminbclassaction (captured
  2026-07-19).
- **Sleep Sound Magnesium contains NO vitamin B6.** Its full active list
  (citrate, glycinate, glycine, hops, calcium citrate — above) has no pyridoxine.
  So the B6 neuropathy matter, on the evidence located, does **not** implicate
  this SKU. Recorded so the two are not conflated — this is a **brand-level**
  context item, not a finding against this product.
- **Separate TGA cancellation:** **"BLACKMORES DAILY MAGNESIUM"** was **Cancelled
  under Section 30(1)(c) of the Act**, date of effect **27 June 2024**, at the
  sponsor's request. Source:
  https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/cancellations-by-sponsors/blackmores-daily-magnesium-cancelled-under-section-301c-act
  (captured 2026-07-19). This is **Daily Magnesium**, a DIFFERENT product, not
  Sleep Sound Magnesium (still current, ARTG 312974). Sponsor-requested
  cancellation, not a safety recall. Recorded for brand completeness.
- **TGA context (2025):** the TGA reported "nearly 240 adverse event reports on
  B6 received in first half of 2025" and issued an interim decision moving
  higher-dose B6 products toward pharmacist-only supply
  (https://www.nutraingredients-asia.com/Article/2025/07/28/tga-received-nearly-240-adverse-event-reports-on-b6-in-first-half-of-2025/).
  A category-wide B6 matter; does not name Sleep Sound Magnesium.
