## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salt:** single-salt **magnesium glycinate** (no blend). This is Swisse's
  "high-absorption" glycinate SKU, distinct from its older **magnesium citrate**
  SKU ("Swisse Ultiboost Magnesium", ARTG/retail 150 mg from magnesium citrate).
- **Elemental disclosed:** yes. Label states verbatim: *"EACH FILM COATED TABLET
  CONTAINS: MAGNESIUM (from magnesium glycinate) 150 mg"*
  (Chemist Warehouse listing, retrieved 2026-07-19;
  https://www.chemistwarehouse.com.au/buy/147527/swisse-ultiboost-magnesium-glycinate-60-tablets).
- **Compound / salt weight disclosed:** **NO.** The glycinate tablet label
  discloses only the elemental figure (150 mg); it does **not** state the
  milligrams of magnesium glycinate compound that deliver it. This is a
  transparency contrast worth flagging, because the sibling **citrate** SKU
  *does* disclose both: *"magnesium citrate 927.64 mg"* providing *"magnesium
  (from magnesium citrate) 150 mg"* (Swisse citrate product page, retrieved
  2026-07-19; https://swisse.com.au/swisse-ultiboost-magnesium). So Swisse
  discloses the compound weight on citrate but not on glycinate.
- **Powder SKU (different dose):** *"Each Scoop (4g) Contains: Magnesium (from
  magnesium glycinate dihydrate) 350 mg"*, unflavoured, and the powder page notes
  *"Contains sulfites"* (Swisse powder page, retrieved 2026-07-19;
  https://swisse.com.au/products/swisse-ultiboost-magnesium-glycinate-powder).
  The 140 g / powder pack is a separate ARTG entry from the tablet and was not
  individually confirmed here.
- No per-form breakdown question applies (single salt, single active).

**Absorption/"high absorption"/"gentle" — supported?** (gathering, not scoring.)
The absorption and gentleness claims are consistently framed as a **comparative**
claim *"when compared to magnesium oxide"* rather than an unqualified "high
absorption" superlative. The comparator (oxide) is named on the label, which a
human reviewer can weigh against the published bioavailability literature at
scoring time. No unqualified "clinically proven" or disease-treatment wording was
observed on the pages captured. The Amazon AU listing title frames it as
*"Supports Mild Muscle Spasm and Healthy Stress Response"*
(https://www.amazon.com.au/Swisse-Ultiboost-Magnesium-Glycinate-Tablets/dp/B0DT1511V8,
retrieved 2026-07-19).

No fear-marketing attacking "fillers" or oxide-as-harmful was observed beyond the
comparative absorption/tolerability framing above.
