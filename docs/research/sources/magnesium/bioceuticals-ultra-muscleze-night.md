## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salt:** magnesium **amino acid chelate**, trademarked **"UltraMag®"**, which
  the manufacturer describes as *"a proprietary blend of highly bioavailable
  magnesium amino acid chelate"* including **magnesium glycinate**
  (bioceuticals.com.au and BioCeuticals FAQ, retrieved 2026-07-20).
- **"UltraMag®" is a trademark for a proprietary blend.** The manufacturer's own
  wording uses **"proprietary blend"** to describe the chelate. The label discloses
  the **UltraMag® compound weight (2.1 g)** and the **elemental total (244 mg)**,
  but does **not** disclose a per-chelate-form breakdown (e.g. how much magnesium
  glycinate vs other amino-acid chelates make up the 2.1 g / 244 mg). **The
  per-form magnesium breakdown is therefore only a combined total, not itemised**
  — this is the "partly undisclosed" transparency point flagged in the brief.
  (Gathering, not scoring; note the additive watchlist's structural
  `proprietary-blend` entry concerns undisclosed *multi-ingredient* blends — here
  each *active* is individually quantified; it is the *chelate-form* split inside
  UltraMag® that is not itemised. Human to characterise.)
- **Elemental disclosed:** **yes** — *"244 mg"* elemental magnesium per 8 g dose.
- **Compound / salt weight disclosed:** **yes for the total** — *"Magnesium amino
  acid chelate UltraMag® 2.1 g (equivalent to 244 mg elemental magnesium)"*
  (manufacturer, retrieved 2026-07-20). **Not disclosed per chelate form.**
- **Per-active breakdown of the combo (disclosed):** the other actives (inositol,
  choline, glycine, vitamin E, hydrolysed milk protein, calcium, sodium) each
  carry an individual quantity (see Additives / full panel). No undisclosed
  proprietary blend of *actives* — each combo active is separately quantified.

Primary regulator here is the **TGA** (AU). Databases checked via ARTG/TGA search

### Vitamin B6 — this product does NOT contain B6 (important, and a contrast to siblings)
- **Ultra Muscleze *Night* contains no vitamin B6.** The BioCeuticals FAQ states
  verbatim: *"No, Ultra Muscleze Night does not contain vitamin B6."* (retrieved
  2026-07-20). B6/pyridoxine does not appear in the Night active list on the
  manufacturer or retailer labels captured.
- **Contrast — the sibling regular "Ultra Muscleze" (non-Night) DOES contain B6.**
  The standard Ultra Muscleze (**AUST L 305455**, sponsor FIT-BioCeuticals,
  reported ARTG start 27 Jun 2018) *"contains pyridoxine hydrochloride"* and carries
  the mandated warning: *"Stop taking this medication if you experience tingling,
  burning or numbness and see your healthcare practitioner as soon as possible
  [contains vitamin B6]"* (search-surfaced, 2026-07-20:
  https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/artg/305455). There is also a **Ultra Muscleze
  P5P** tablet variant (P5P = pyridoxal-5-phosphate, an activated B6 form). **So
  the Ultra Muscleze *family* is a B6-containing range; the *Night* SKU is the
  B6-free member.** A human comparing SKUs must not conflate them.

### TGA whole-of-market vitamin B6 / peripheral-neuropathy action (context; applies to the family, NOT to Night's own formula)
- TGA safety review: *"Peripheral neuropathy with supplementary vitamin B6
  (pyridoxine)"* —
  https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/peripheral-neuropathy-supplementary-vitamin-b6-pyridoxine
- TGA safety alert: *"Health supplements containing vitamin B6 can cause peripheral
  neuropathy"* —
  https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/health-supplements-containing-vitamin-b6-can-cause-peripheral-neuropathy
- Key TGA facts (from search of these pages, 2026-07-20): up to **5 Aug 2022** the
  TGA had *"32 adverse event reports with sufficient information to establish a
  possible causal association between peripheral neuropathy and products
  containing vitamin B6,"* and *"in many cases, people reported they were unaware
  they had consumed vitamin B6 as the product they were taking was a magnesium
  supplement."* The TGA notes *"peripheral neuropathy may occur at a daily dose of
  less than 50 mg of vitamin B6."* Since **1 March 2022**, medicines with a daily
  dose of vitamin B6 **over 10 mg** must carry the peripheral-neuropathy warning
  (previously only over 50 mg).
  - **Relevance to Ultra Muscleze Night:** because **Night contains no B6**, the
    B6 warning does **not** apply to it — a point in its favour on the exact AU
    theme that has dogged magnesium+B6 sleep powders. The B6 theme is captured here
    because (a) the brief flagged it and (b) the sibling *Ultra Muscleze* /
    *Ultra Muscleze P5P* SKUs in the same family DO carry B6 and the mandated
    warning. **No B6-overage recall specific to any BioCeuticals Muscleze product
    was found** (checked 2026-07-20) — the well-known B6-overage powder recall in
    AU was a *different* brand family (Ethical Nutrients / Metagenics, Raspberry
    powder batch), not BioCeuticals. Human to confirm.

study is cited on the pages captured. No overt fear-marketing attacking "fillers"
was observed; the absorption framing references magnesium **oxide** only, via the
footnote. **"Proprietary blend"** is used by the manufacturer itself for UltraMag®.
