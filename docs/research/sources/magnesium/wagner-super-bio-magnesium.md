## B6 STATUS (CRITICAL AU AXIS) — CONTAINS B6, on-pack neuropathy warning present

**This product CONTAINS vitamin B6.** This is a defining fact; it sits in the AU
peripheral-neuropathy risk zone described in `_findings.md`.

- **Ingredient:** **Pyridoxine hydrochloride 50 mg per tablet** (Chemist
  Warehouse label listing + ARTG active-ingredient list, 2026-07-21).
- **Elemental B6:** the retailer listing states **"Pyridoxine hydrochloride 50 mg
  (Vitamin B6 equiv. 41 mg)"** — i.e. **~41 mg elemental vitamin B6 per tablet.**
- **B6 per day at label directions:** at **1 tablet/day**, that is **pyridoxine
  HCl 50 mg/day ≈ 41 mg elemental B6/day.** Below the 100 mg/day TGA adult maximum,
  but still a daily supplemental B6 load carrying the mandated warning. Recorded
  as a fact; not scored.
- **On-pack neuropathy warning:** **PRESENT.** Verbatim from the Chemist Warehouse
  listing (2026-07-21): > "Stop taking this medication if you experience tingling,
  burning or numbness and see your healthcare practitioner as soon as possible."
  This is the TGA-mandated B6 neuropathy warning.
- **Cross-reference:** an AJGP (RACGP) August 2025 case study on **pyridoxine
  toxicity from over-the-counter supplements** underscores why the AU B6 axis
  matters for magnesium+B6 tablets like this one (see Open questions #4). Recorded
  as B6-axis context, not a finding against this SKU.
