## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Which magnesium salt?** ONE salt: **magnesium carbonate hydrate.** Stated
  verbatim on the manufacturer page and every retailer as **"Magnesium (from
  Magnesium Carbonate Hydrate) 320mg"** (manufacturer,
  https://www.naturesown.com.au/products/sleep-support/sleep-magnesium/, captured
  2026-07-19; Chemist Warehouse 20-pack,
  https://www.chemistwarehouse.com.au/buy/124288/nature-s-own-magnesium-sleep-effervescent-20-tablets,
  captured 2026-07-19).
- **Elemental vs compound disclosed?** **Yes — elemental is disclosed.** The
  label expresses the active as elemental magnesium ("Magnesium … 320mg") and
  names the source compound ("from Magnesium Carbonate Hydrate"). This is the
  clearer of the two conventions (it does NOT quote a large compound weight and
  let the reader assume it is elemental). Single salt, so there is **no hidden
  per-form blend** — the per-form breakdown question that dogs the multi-salt
  products in this pilot (Caruso's, Herbs of Gold, BioCeuticals) does not arise
  here. Recorded as: single-salt, elemental-stated, no blend-transparency defect.
- **Passionflower quantification:** disclosed as extract weight + herb
  equivalent — "Passiflora incarnata dry flower extract 2.5mg from 250mg dry
  flower" (manufacturer page, captured 2026-07-19). Itemised, not a blend.
- **Effervescent delivery note (form fact, not a score):** dissolved in 200 mL
  water; a carbonate + acid effervescent base generates CO₂ and yields a
  lemon-balm-flavoured drink. The effervescent base is the reason the product
  carries a notable **sodium** load and acid/sweetener excipients (Additives).
- **Bioavailability note (form fact only, NOT a watchlist matter):** magnesium
  carbonate is a comparatively low-solubility inorganic salt; like oxide, this is
  a **form/bioavailability fact**, recorded here as a form attribute, NOT as an
  additive flag (per README: "Note magnesium oxide only as a form/bioavailability
  fact"). In an effervescent, the carbonate reacts with the acid base in water,
  which changes the delivered species — a human should note this at scoring time
  rather than treat carbonate like a swallowed carbonate tablet.
