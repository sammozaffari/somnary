**Status:** candidate list for owner review; human-gated so selection can't be accused of
cherry-picking. Sourced by a research agent (2026-07-19), trimmed to a 12-product pilot set.
No product here is scored — this is only *which products we research*.

## Inclusion criteria, applied evenly

- **Magnesium positioned for sleep/relaxation/muscle/nervous-system support** — the framing
  magnesium is sold under in AU. Single-ingredient prioritized; a bounded few sleep combos
  (magnesium + glycine/herbs/L-theanine) included because AU sleep-magnesium so often
  combines — each labeled `combo`.
- **AU availability is the filter** (magnesium is OTC here, unlike melatonin): Chemist
  Warehouse + Amazon AU for the AU brands; iHerb AU for the imported contrast set. AUST L
  (TGA listing) captured where present — a real sourcing signal (imports carry none).
- **Form spanned on purpose:** glycinate/bisglycinate, citrate, oxide, carbonate, orotate,
  amino-acid chelate, and multi-salt "high-absorption" blends. Where products blend several
  salts, the **per-form elemental breakdown disclosed vs hidden** is the differentiator.
- **Quality spectrum, evenly reached:** mainstream AU chemist (Swisse, Blackmores, Nature's
  Own, Bioglan, Caruso's), practitioner-tier AU (Ethical Nutrients, Herbs of Gold,
  BioCeuticals, Cabot Health), and an iHerb-imported contrast (Doctor's Best, Thorne).

## Deliberate exclusions (documented)

- **High-strength oxide "1000mg" cheap tablets** (e.g. Nature's Way / generic oxide) — the
  oxide form is already represented (Bioglan, Caruso's); adding more would pad the low end.
- **Topicals (creams/sprays/flakes) and magnesium gummies** — outside the oral-supplement
  scope for this pilot.
- **Cenovis** — only low-differentiation oxide SKUs surfaced.
- Bench (verified alternates if a slot drops): NOW Magnesium Glycinate, NOW Magnesium
  Citrate, Pure Encapsulations Magnesium Glycinate (all iHerb AU).
