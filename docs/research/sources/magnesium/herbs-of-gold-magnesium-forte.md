## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salts:** FOUR magnesium salts in a single tablet — a multi-salt blend, but
  magnesium-only (one mineral across four sources).
- **PER-FORM elemental breakdown — FULLY DISCLOSED.** This is a transparency
  high-water mark for the magnesium set: the label states each salt AND its
  elemental contribution, not just a combined total. Quoted verbatim from the
  Herbs of Gold product page and corroborating retailer/TGA-search text
  (herbsofgold.com.au/products/magnesium-forte and Chemist Direct, retrieved
  2026-07-19):
  - *"Magnesium amino acid chelate equiv. magnesium 150mg"*
  - *"Magnesium citrate equiv. magnesium 50mg"*
  - *"Magnesium glycinate equiv. magnesium 15mg"*
  - *"Magnesium orotate dihydrate equiv. magnesium 5mg"*
  - *"TOTAL ELEMENTAL MAGNESIUM 220mg"*
- **Elemental vs compound weight:** elemental magnesium is disclosed per salt
  (the "equiv. magnesium" figures above sum to 220 mg). The *compound* (raw salt)
  milligram weights are NOT printed — i.e. it does not state how many mg of
  magnesium citrate deliver the 50 mg elemental, etc. So the disclosure is
  **per-form ELEMENTAL** (fully broken out), but not per-form compound weight.
  This is still materially more transparent than a single combined "elemental
  220 mg" figure, and far more than a hidden blend.
- **Hidden-blend defect:** NONE. Because each of the four salts has its own
  disclosed elemental contribution, this is not a proprietary/undisclosed blend.
  A human can see exactly how the 220 mg is apportioned across the four forms
  (150 / 50 / 15 / 5).
- The four "equiv. magnesium" figures (150 + 50 + 15 + 5) reconcile exactly to
  the stated 220 mg total — internal consistency check passes.

## Vitamin B6 (AU regulatory theme — checked carefully)

- **Contains B6? NO.** The live TGA ARTG 465031 ingredient list names only four
  ingredients — *"magnesium amino acid chelate, magnesium citrate, magnesium
  glycinate, magnesium orotate dihydrate"* — with **no pyridoxine / vitamin B6**
  (TGA ARTG 465031, read live 2026-07-19,
  https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/artg/465031). B6 / pyridoxine does not appear
  anywhere in the manufacturer or any retailer active-ingredient panel checked
  (herbsofgold.com.au, GoVita, Chemist Direct, Healthylife, Nourished Life,
  Australian Vitamins), checked 2026-07-19.
- **Why this matters (context, not a score):** Many AU "Magnesium + B6" products
  were caught up in the TGA's vitamin B6 / peripheral-neuropathy action. Per the
  TGA safety review, *"in many cases, people reported they were unaware they had
  consumed vitamin B6 as the product they were taking was a magnesium
  supplement"*; since **1 March 2022** listed medicines with a daily B6 dose
  **over 10 mg** must carry a peripheral-neuropathy warning statement, and a
  further scheduling change (announced) restricts higher B6 doses from **1 June
  2027** (TGA, "Health supplements containing vitamin B6 can cause peripheral
  neuropathy" and "Medicines containing vitamin B6…", retrieved 2026-07-19:
  https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/health-supplements-containing-vitamin-b6-can-cause-peripheral-neuropathy
  ; https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/medicines-containing-vitamin-b6-pyridoxine-pyridoxal-or-pyridoxamine).
- **Bottom line on the "Forte" B6 trap:** the brief flagged that "Forte" versions
  of magnesium products *sometimes* add B6. **This particular "Forte" does NOT.**
  Herbs of Gold "Magnesium Forte" derives its "forte" from the 4-salt / 220 mg
  elemental load, not from added B6. (Note a *sibling* Herbs of Gold SKU,
  "Magnesium Powder High Strength", is a separate product that *does* contain B6 —
  do not conflate it with this tablet.) No B6-related warning statement therefore
  applies to Magnesium Forte, and it is not exposed to the B6 cancellation wave.
