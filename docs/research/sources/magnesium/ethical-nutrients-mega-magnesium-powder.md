## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salt:** single branded magnesium **diglycinate** (bisglycinate) — a fully
  reacted amino-acid chelate. Marketed under the trademark **Meta Mag®** (and, on
  the current manufacturer page, **MagActive®** — see wording note).
- **"Meta Mag" is a trademark.** Captured descriptions (retrieved 2026-07-19):
  - Retail listings describe the active as *"Magnesium amino acid chelate (Meta
    Mag - Magnesium diglycinate) 3.0g equiv. elemental Magnesium 300mg"* and
    *"Magnesium glycinate dihydrate (Meta Mag® - Magnesium diglycinate) 3g Equiv.
    Magnesium 300mg"* (Pharmacy Online 450 g and Blooms The Chemist listings,
    retrieved 2026-07-19).
  - The **current manufacturer / Chemist Warehouse** label instead reads:
    *"MagActive® magnesium diglycinate blend: Magnesium glycinate dihydrate 1 g,
    Magnesium glycinate 1.3 g (Equiv. Magnesium 300 mg)"*
    (https://www.metagenics.com.au/products/ethical-nutrients-mega-magnesium-citrus-200g-powder
    and
    https://www.chemistwarehouse.com.au/buy/72517/ethical-nutrients-mega-magnesium-200g-powder-citrus,
    retrieved 2026-07-19). **This is a branding change** ("Meta Mag" → "MagActive")
    and a slightly different label breakdown (a blend of magnesium glycinate
    dihydrate 1 g + magnesium glycinate 1.3 g) — a human should confirm whether
    the retail listings quoting "Meta Mag" are older label copy for the same
    formula. The word **"blend"** now appears in the active line.
- **Elemental disclosed:** **yes.** All captured labels give the elemental figure
  (*"Equiv. Magnesium 300 mg"* / *"equiv. elemental Magnesium 300mg"*).
- **Compound / salt weight disclosed:** **partially / mixed.**
  - Retail listings disclose a **combined compound weight** (*"...3.0g equiv.
    elemental Magnesium 300mg"* / *"...3g Equiv. Magnesium 300mg"*) — i.e. 3 g of
    magnesium diglycinate delivering 300 mg elemental.
  - The current manufacturer label breaks the 3 g into two components
    (*"Magnesium glycinate dihydrate 1 g, Magnesium glycinate 1.3 g"*) which sum
    to 2.3 g, not 3 g — the arithmetic between the "3 g compound" retail wording
    and the "1 g + 1.3 g" manufacturer wording does not obviously reconcile.
    **Human to verify** the exact current compound breakdown against the live
    ARTG entry.
- **Per-active breakdown of the combo (disclosed):** all actives carry individual
  quantities (see Additives / full panel). No undisclosed proprietary blend of

- **Recall: Ethical Nutrients Mega Magnesium Raspberry Powder** — sponsor

  burning and numbness."* Distributed nationwide.
  Source (search-snippet; live TGA page timed out 2026-07-19):
  https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/product-recalls/recall-ethical-nutrients-mega-magnesium-raspberry-powder

    sibling flavour (Raspberry) in the same Mega Magnesium *powder* range, over

    powder itself was not the recalled SKU, but the recall shows a B6 label-vs-
    content failure occurred in this powder family. **Human to confirm** the

### TGA class action on vitamin B6 (whole-of-market, directly applies)
- TGA safety review: *"Peripheral neuropathy with supplementary vitamin B6
  (pyridoxine)"* —
  https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/peripheral-neuropathy-supplementary-vitamin-b6-pyridoxine
  and *"Health supplements containing vitamin B6 can cause peripheral
  neuropathy"* —
  https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/health-supplements-containing-vitamin-b6-can-cause-peripheral-neuropathy
- Key TGA facts (from search of these pages, 2026-07-19): up to 5 Aug 2022 the TGA
  had *"32 adverse event reports with sufficient information to establish a
  possible causal association between peripheral neuropathy and products
  containing vitamin B6,"* and *"in many cases, people reported they were unaware
  they had consumed vitamin B6 as the product they were taking was a magnesium
  supplement."* Since **1 March 2022**, medicines with a daily dose of vitamin B6
  **over 10 mg** must carry a peripheral-neuropathy warning (previously only over
  50 mg); products **must not provide more than 100 mg B6/day** for adults
  (previously 200 mg), with a transition period to **1 March 2023**.
  **This product delivers 50 mg B6 per 8.7 g dose → 100 mg/day at the label's
  therapeutic twice-daily use**, i.e. it sits at the new adult maximum, and it is
  precisely the "magnesium supplement people didn't know contained B6" category
  the TGA flagged. (Gathering, not scoring.)
