## Form & dose transparency (magnesium-specific)

- **Salts:** three — **heavy magnesium oxide + magnesium phosphate pentahydrate +
  magnesium glycinate** (a multi-salt COMBO). This is an **oxide-heavy blend**:
  100 of the 225 elemental mg (≈44%) come from magnesium **oxide**, the lowest-
  solubility/lowest-bioavailability common form; a further 100 mg from phosphate
  pentahydrate; and only 25 mg from the glycinate (the form most marketed for
  bioavailability/tolerability). Recorded as a **form/bioavailability fact**, not
  a watchlist additive (per README + watchlist scope).
- **PER-FORM elemental breakdown — DISCLOSED (verbatim active panel, Chemist
  Warehouse + bioglan.com.au + retailers, captured 2026-07-19):**
  > "Each Film Coated Tablet Contains: Magnesium (as Heavy magnesium oxide 165.84mg) 100mg / Magnesium (as Magnesium phosphate pentahydrate 484.05mg) 100mg / Magnesium (as Magnesium glycinate 208.33mg) 25mg / Total elemental Magnesium 225mg / Humulus lupulus (Hops) flower ext. dry conc. 50mg equiv. to flower dry 500mg / Eschscholzia californica (California Poppy) herb flowering ext. dry conc. 166.67mg equiv to. herb flowering dry 1000mg"
- **Elemental vs compound weight:** **BOTH disclosed for every salt.** The label
  states the **compound weight** (oxide 165.84 mg, phosphate pentahydrate 484.05 mg,
  glycinate 208.33 mg) AND the **elemental yield** (100 / 100 / 25 mg) for each,
  plus the summed total (225 mg). This is a **strong transparency signal**: there
  is no hidden combined-only total and no proprietary-blend concealment of the
  per-salt split.
- **Herb doses disclosed (both raw-herb equivalent AND extract concentrate):**
  - **Hops (Humulus lupulus)** flower — extract dry concentrate **50 mg**, equiv.
    to flower dry **500 mg**.
  - **California poppy (Eschscholzia californica)** herb flowering — extract dry
    concentrate **166.67 mg**, equiv. to herb flowering dry **1000 mg**.
- **Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine):** **ABSENT.** No pyridoxine hydrochloride, pyridoxal
  5-phosphate, or any B6 form appears in the active panel, and the TGA ARTG
  ingredient list (353356) names only Eschscholzia californica, heavy magnesium
  oxide, Humulus lupulus, magnesium glycinate, magnesium phosphate pentahydrate —
  no B6. Confirmed against three retailer panels + the ARTG record on 2026-07-19.
  (See the Regulatory record for why B6 absence matters for AU magnesium SKUs.)

the TGA has cancelled/relabelled several B6-containing magnesium products and the

apply to it — a point of note (B6 absence removes the peripheral-neuropathy risk
vector that affects many rival AU magnesium sleep tablets).
- **TGA safety alert (primary), "Health supplements containing vitamin B6 can
  cause peripheral neuropathy", published 10 November 2022**
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/health-supplements-containing-vitamin-b6-can-cause-peripheral-neuropathy),
  quoted verbatim (read in-browser 2026-07-19):
  > "Taking vitamin B6 even at low doses can cause peripheral neuropathy but people are more likely to get it if they are taking more than one supplement."
  > "The TGA has received over 30 reports of peripheral neuropathy suspected to be related to products containing vitamin B6."
  > "In many cases, people reported they were not aware they had consumed vitamin B6, as the product they were taking was a magnesium supplement."
  > "products containing a daily dose of vitamin B6 above 10 mg require a warning about peripheral neuropathy" ... "the maximum permitted daily dose of vitamin B6 in individual products has been reduced from 200 mg to 100 mg for adults"
- **Relevance to this SKU:** none as a compliance matter (no B6 present). Recorded
  so a human reviewer can note the *comparative* transparency/safety advantage:
