### 1. New York Attorney General — cease-and-desist + nationwide agreement (herbal supplements; NBTY)

- **event_type:** state-AG enforcement (cease-and-desist, then settlement agreement)
- **agency:** New York State Office of the Attorney General
- **event_date:** cease-and-desist letters **Feb 3, 2015**; NBTY agreement **Sep 28, 2016**
- **primary documents:**
  - https://ag.ny.gov/press-release/2015/ag-schneiderman-asks-major-retailers-halt-sales-certain-herbal-supplements-dna
  - https://ag.ny.gov/press-release/2016/ag-schneiderman-announces-major-nationwide-agreement-nbty-herbal-supplement-maker
- **quoted (2015 press release title):** "A.G. Schneiderman Asks Major Retailers To Halt Sales Of Certain Herbal Supplements As DNA Tests Fail To Detect Plant Materials Listed On Majority Of Products Tested"
- **quoted (2015 finding):** "Overall, just 21% of the test results from store brand herbal supplements verified DNA from the plants listed on the products' labels — with 79% coming up empty."
- **quoted (2016 agreement title):** "A.G. Schneiderman Announces Major Nationwide Agreement With NBTY, Herbal Supplement Maker for Walgreens And Walmart"
- **quoted (2016 finding):** "a study commissioned by the office failed to detect identifiable genetic material for the plants depicted on the labels in most of the four retailers' herbal supplement products"
- **quoted (2016, what NBTY agreed to):** to "implement DNA barcoding on herbal ingredients within two years"; "implement an annual testing protocol to detect allergens (peanuts, milk, soy, eggs, and wheat)"; "double the number of on-site audits of major ingredient suppliers"; and "invest no less than $250,000 in herbal authenticity genetic research"
- **quoted (Attorney General):** "When consumers take an herbal supplement, they should be able to do so confident that the plant on the label is the plant in the bottle, and that every reasonable precaution was taken to ensure the product's authenticity and purity."
- **scope note (no characterization):** the action concerned **store-brand herbal** supplements manufactured by NBTY (sold under Walgreens/Walmart house brands). It did not name this melatonin product. Recorded here because NBTY is the manufacturer/parent of this brand.

### 2. Pearson v. NBTY, Inc. — glucosamine/chondroitin consumer class action (private litigation, not FTC)

- **event_type:** federal consumer class action (private; **not** an FTC or FDA action)
- **court/citation:** *Pearson v. NBTY, Inc.*, **772 F.3d 778 (7th Cir. 2014)**, decided **Nov 19, 2014**
- **named entities:** NBTY, Inc.; subsidiary **Rexall Sundown, Inc.**
- **primary/court documents:** https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/us-7th-circuit/1684622.html (Justia mirror https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca7/14-1389/14-1389-2014-11-19.html returned HTTP 403 on fetch 2026-07-19)
- **quoted (court characterization of the settlement):** "a selfish deal between class counsel and the defendant" that "disserves the class"
- **claims at issue (as recorded in the opinion):** glucosamine/chondroitin joint-health marketing claims including "help rebuild cartilage," "support renewal of cartilage," "maintain the structural integrity of joints," "lubricate joints," and "support mobility and flexibility"
- **scope note (no characterization):** concerns **glucosamine/chondroitin** joint products, not melatonin. Recorded because the defendant is the manufacturer/parent (NBTY) of this brand. This is private class-action litigation over the fairness of a class settlement; it is not a regulator finding of product mislabeling.
