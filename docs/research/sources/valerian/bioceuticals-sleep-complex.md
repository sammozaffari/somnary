## Standardisation & dose transparency (valerian-specific) — RARE FOR A COMBO: a per-tablet valerenic-acid mg IS disclosed

**This product DOES print a per-tablet valerenic-acid mg (1.13 mg) — the key
valerian sourcing signal — which most COMBO products drop.** Captured verbatim.

**On-label active panel** (verbatim, Chemist Warehouse & Your Discount Chemist,
2026-07-20; note both reproduce the vendor's typo "extracy"):

> "Herbals: Zizyphus jujuba var. spinosa 450 mg equiv. dry seed 4.5 g /
> Valeriana officinalis (valerian) extracy dry conc. 375 mg equiv. to dry root
> 1.5 g std. to contain valerenic acid 1.13 mg / Essential oil: Lavandula
> angustifolia (lavender oil) 2 mg."

**Transparency reading (recorded, not scored):**
- **Per-tablet valerenic-acid mg IS disclosed: 1.13 mg/tablet** (2.26 mg at the
  2-tablet dose). This is the README's strongest standardisation signal ("standardised
  to a stated valerenic acid % or mg per serving") — and it is **present**, which is
  **rare for a combo** in this universe (contrast MediHerb Valerian Complex, which
  discloses NO per-tablet valerenic-acid mg).
- **Extract concentrate + dry-root equivalent disclosed:** valerian **375 mg of a
  dry concentrate = 1.5 g (1500 mg) dry-root equivalent** per tablet — i.e. an
  effective **~4:1 extract**. Zizyphus **450 mg = 4.5 g dry-seed equivalent** (~10:1).
- **Valerian dose is DISCLOSED, not buried:** each active carries its own quantity
  and dry-herb equivalent; there is no single blended "proprietary" weight. For a
  combo, the valerian contribution is fully legible.
- **Named trade extract:** the valerian extract is **Bio-156**, which marketing copy
  describes as "phyto-equivalent to the clinically trialled LI-156" (see Marketing
  claims). LI-156 is the code for the standardised valerian extract Sedonium (used in
  published valerian sleep RCTs). The label itself states the standardisation as an
  absolute mg (1.13 mg valerenic acid), NOT as a % — capture both framings.
- **Minor cross-source discrepancy (recorded):** Natural Chemist renders the zizyphus
  dry-seed equivalent as **"4.65 g"** rather than 4.5 g
  (https://www.naturalchemist.com.au/bioceuticals-sleep-complex-60-tablets/, 2026-07-20);
  the manufacturer page, Chemist Warehouse and Your Discount Chemist all show **4.5 g**.
  A human should confirm against the physical pack.
- **Net:** a fully-quantified combo that — unusually — retains the per-tablet
  valerenic-acid marker mg (1.13 mg), discloses the extract concentrate and dry-root
  equivalent (375 mg = 1.5 g), and names its trade extract (Bio-156). The strongest
  standardisation signal is PRESENT, not absent.
