## Used in published trials (→ display flag) — THE HEADLINE SIGNAL

**YES — this exact named extract (Ze 91019) has multiple published trials.** The
extract in ReDormin Forte / Redormin 500 *is* Ze 91019 (Max Zeller Söhne AG), and
Ze 91019 is the subject of a published clinical/mechanistic literature. This is a
genuine "used in published trials" display flag; cite these (verify each says what
the marketing implies before any efficacy claim is surfaced):

1. **Füssel A, Wolf A, Brattström A. "Effect of a fixed valerian-Hop extract
   combination (Ze 91019) on sleep polygraphy in patients with non-organic
   insomnia: a pilot study." Eur J Med Res. 2000;5(9):385-390. PMID 11003973.**
   Polysomnography pilot, 30 patients, 2 weeks; reported reduced sleep latency and
   wake time, increased slow-wave sleep. (Tablet used: 250 mg valerian + 60 mg
   hops × 2 = same total as the 500 mg/120 mg Forte tablet.)
2. **Koetter U, Schrader E, Käufeler R, Brattström A. "A randomized, double blind,
   placebo-controlled, prospective clinical study to demonstrate clinical efficacy
   of a fixed valerian hops extract combination (Ze 91019) in patients suffering
   from non-organic sleep disorder." Phytother Res. 2007;21(9):847-851.
   PMID 17486686. DOI 10.1002/ptr.2167.** 4-week RCT; the **fixed valerian-hops
   combination was significantly superior to placebo in reducing sleep latency,
   while single valerian (Ze 911) was NOT** — the key result cited to justify the
   combination.
3. **Schellenberg R, Sauer S, Abourashed EA, Koetter U, Brattström A. "The fixed
   combination of valerian and hops (Ze 91019) acts via a central adenosine
   mechanism." Planta Med. 2004;70(7):594-597. PMID 15254851.
   DOI 10.1055/s-2004-827180.** Mechanistic RCT (caffeine-challenge EEG);
   proposes a central adenosine mechanism.
4. **Brattström A. "Scientific evidence for a fixed extract combination (Ze 91019)
   from valerian and hops traditionally used as a sleep-inducing aid." Wien Med
   Wochenschr. 2007;157(13-14):367-370. PMID 17704989.
   DOI 10.1007/s10354-007-0442-6.** Manufacturer-authored review (Max Zeller Söhne
   AG affiliation — note conflict of interest when weighing).
5. **Schicktanz N, Gerhards C, Schlitt T, et al. "Effects of a Valerian-Hops
   Extract Combination (Ze 91019) on Sleep Duration and Daytime Cognitive and
   Psychological Parameters in Occasional Insomnia: A Randomized Controlled
   Feasibility Trial." Brain Behav. 2025;15(6):e70600. PMID 40462685.
   DOI 10.1002/brb3.70600.** Recent RCT feasibility trial explicitly using
   **"Redormin 500" (each tablet 500 mg valerian + 120 mg hops)** — i.e. THIS
   product formula; reported +21.7 min mean nightly sleep duration vs placebo.
   Pre-registered as **ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05684523**
   (https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05684523; official title "Feasibility
   Study on the Use of Redormin® 500 (Ze 91019) on Day-time Cognition and Quality
   of Life in People With Occasional Sleep Problems"; lead sponsor **Max Zeller
   Soehne AG**; status COMPLETED).

**Reading for a human (recorded, not scored):** Ze 91019 is the clearest
"used-in-published-trials" case in this valerian universe — the studied extract IS
the product (esp. the 2025 trial, which names "Redormin 500" and its exact 500/120
mg tablet, with a ClinicalTrials.gov registration). Caveats to weigh at scoring:
(a) the trials are **overwhelmingly manufacturer-sponsored / -authored** (Max
Zeller Söhne AG), which is a conflict-of-interest signal, not an independence
signal; (b) sample sizes are modest (30–40 patients in the pilot and 2025 trial);
