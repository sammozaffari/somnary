## Standardisation & dose transparency (valerian-specific) — A DIFFERENT QUALITY-SIGNALLING MODEL

**This product does NOT print a per-tablet valerenic-acid mg or %.** It signals
quality through two separate, distinct mechanisms — capture both precisely:

**1) On-label: extract ratio + dry-herb-equivalent (DHE), per tablet.** Quoted
verbatim from the target NutriMedica listing
(https://nutrimedica.com.au/products/mediherb-valerian-complex-60-tablets),
2026-07-20:

> "Valerian root & rhizome 5:1 extract DHE: 700 mg from *Valeriana officinalis*
> root & rhizome — 140 mg"
> "Passion Flower herb 5:1 extract DHE: 500 mg from *Passiflora incarnata* herb —
> 100 mg"
> "Spiny Jujube seed 10:1 extract DHE: 900 mg from *Zizyphus Spinosa* seed — 90 mg"

Corroborated on the Standard Process US page (per tablet): "Valerian root &
rhizome 5:1 extract 140 mg (from *Valeriana officinalis* root & rhizome 700 mg),
Passion Flower herb 5:1 extract 100 mg (from *Passiflora incarnata* herb 500 mg),
Jujube seed 10:1 extract 90 mg (from *Ziziphus jujuba var. spinosa* seed 900 mg)"
plus "Calcium 64 mg" (https://www.standardprocess.com/products/valerian-complex,
2026-07-20).

**2) Brand-level (not per-batch, not per-tablet): an HPLC quality claim** on the
Standard Process / MediHerb product description. Quoted verbatim
(https://www.standardprocess.com/products/valerian-complex, 2026-07-20):

> "MediHerb has developed a unique HPLC analytical method to determine the levels
> of valerenic acids and valepotriates in Valerian… The method can also determine
> the level of the baldrinals (valtrate degradation products) which are an
> indicator of a poor quality herb. By using this analytical method on all its
> Valerian products, MediHerb ensures that these products contain high levels of
> valerenic acids and valepotriates, with no baldrinals."

**Transparency reading (recorded, not scored):**
- **NO per-tablet valerenic-acid mg is disclosed** — unlike the AU single-herb
  Forte products, which print an absolute mg (e.g. Blackmores 3.2 mg/tablet). Here
  the strongest README signal ("standardised to a stated valerenic acid % or mg
  per serving") is **ABSENT as a numeric label figure.**
- **Instead**, the actives are disclosed as **extract ratio (5:1 valerian, 5:1
  passionflower, 10:1 jujube) + DHE (700 / 500 / 900 mg)** per tablet — a
  transparent, per-ingredient (not proprietary-blend) disclosure, but a
  ratio/DHE model, not a marker-mg model.
- The **HPLC claim is a BRAND-LEVEL / process assurance** ("on all its Valerian
  products… high levels of valerenic acids and valepotriates, with no
  baldrinals"). It is a **qualitative** assurance ("high levels", "no baldrinals")
  — **not** a quantified per-batch or per-tablet valerenic-acid figure, and **not**
  a published per-lot assay result. Capture it as a marketing/quality claim, not as
  a disclosed standardisation number.
- **Species named:** *Valeriana officinalis* (root & rhizome), *Passiflora
  incarnata* (herb), *Ziziphus jujuba var. spinosa* / "Zizyphus Spinosa" (seed).
- **Valerian dose per serving is disclosed and unambiguous (700 mg DHE/tablet)**;
  the **daily** dose is a practitioner-directed range (2–4 tablets/day depending on
  source), not a fixed one-a-day.
- **Net:** a DIFFERENT quality-signalling model — extract-ratio + DHE on-label,
  plus a brand-level qualitative HPLC assurance — vs. the label-valerenic-acid-mg
  model of the mainstream single-herb Fortes. Neither a per-tablet marker mg nor a
  published per-batch assay was located for this SKU (2026-07-20).
