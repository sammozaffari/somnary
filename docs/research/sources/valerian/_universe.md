**Status:** candidate list for owner review; human-gated so selection can't be accused of
cherry-picking. Sourced by a research agent (2026-07-20), trimmed to a 12-product pilot. No product
here is scored — this is only *which products we research*.

## Inclusion criteria, applied evenly
- Valerian (*Valeriana officinalis*) a lead ingredient positioned for sleep. Single-herb prioritised;
  a bounded set of common AU sleep combos (valerian + hops/passionflower/ziziphus/magnesium) admitted,
  each labeled `combo`.
- **Standardisation is the quality axis** (valerian's analogue of magnesium "form"): standardised to
  valerenic acid %/mg? root vs extract? extract ratio / dry-root equivalent? Non-disclosure recorded.
- Availability: Chemist Warehouse / Amazon AU (AU brands) + iHerb AU (imports). AUST L captured where
  found (imports carry none).
- Quality spectrum evenly: mainstream AU chemist, practitioner-tier AU, iHerb-imported contrast.

## Deliberate exclusions
- **Fusion Health "Sleep"** — its sleep formula (ziziphus + albizia + passionflower) contains NO
  valerian; fails the lead-ingredient test despite the brand's sleep positioning.
- **Thorne** — no standalone valerian product.
- **Herbs of Gold, Brauer, Bioglan** — valerian appears only inside combos whose per-tablet valerian
  dose couldn't be confirmed on a retail page this pass; held rather than guess a dose.
- Dropped from the agent's 14: an unconfirmed Nature's Way valerian+hops combo (no confirmed SKU/dose)
  and a second redundant whole-root import.

## Notes for the owner
- **The built-in test:** four single-herb products at ~2000 mg dry-root — Blackmores, Caruso's,
  Thompson's DISCLOSE valerenic acid; Nature's Own does NOT. Same dose, different transparency — the
  cleanest standardisation-disclosure contrast in the set.
- **Flordis ReDormin Forte** uses the trial-studied Ze 91019 extract — flag at scoring whether a
  "named clinically-studied extract" is a distinct higher signal (like Thorne's NSF cert was for mag).
- **Capture gap:** AUST L numbers confirmed only for Blackmores Valerian Forte (ARTG 351518/435091)
  and Flordis ReDormin Forte (AUST L 283649); the rest need ARTG confirmation before scoring.
- Rubric question to settle at scoring (like magnesium's oxide/hidden-blend): does UNSTANDARDISED
  whole-root (or non-disclosure of valerenic acid) affect a SOURCING score, or is potency an
  evidence/efficacy matter? (Recommend: non-disclosure → Transparency; potency of the form → evidence
  grade, not a sourcing penalty — mirroring the magnesium oxide ruling.)
