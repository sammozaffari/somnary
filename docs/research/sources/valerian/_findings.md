# Valerian dossiers — consolidation & owner decisions (AU pilot)

Research complete for all 12 candidates (2026-07-20). **Gather-only: no scores drafted.** Read
before the scoring pass.

## The central valerian story: standardisation disclosure

Valerian's quality axis (like "form" for magnesium) is whether the label discloses how much active
constituent (**valerenic acid**) you're actually getting. The set splits cleanly, and the flagship
finding is a same-dose transparency gap:

- **The "Forte 2000" test** — four single-herb products at ~2000 mg dry-root: **Blackmores (3.2 mg),
  Caruso's (4.5 mg), Thompson's (3.56 mg) DISCLOSE valerenic acid; Nature's Own does NOT** — same
  dose, same shelf, but the only one that omits the active-constituent figure. The clean, non-obvious
  transparency story.
- **Combos usually drop the marker** — Blackmores Deep Sleep and Nature's Own Sleep Ezy disclose no
  valerenic acid (though they disclose the valerian *dose*, itemised, not hidden). **BioCeuticals
  Sleep Complex is the exception** — the one combo that keeps the valerenic-acid figure (1.13 mg).
- **Swisse Sleep DOES standardise (2.6 mg) but discloses it inconsistently** — printed on Amazon AU,
  omitted on Chemist Warehouse and swisse.com.au (the info exists but not on the main retail pages).
- **MediHerb** uses a different, legitimate model — no marker mg, but discloses extract ratios (5:1,
  10:1), dry-herb equivalents, a full excipient panel, and a brand HPLC assurance.
- **Imports:** NOW = unstandardised WHOLE ROOT (no extract, no valerenic acid — the reader gets root
  powder with no active guarantee); Gaia = standardised (1.8 mg) **and** publishes per-batch lab
  results (see below).

## The transparency/testing standout: Gaia (an import)

**Gaia Herbs** is the valerian analogue of Thorne-for-magnesium: the only product in the set that
**publishes per-batch lab results** ("Meet Your Herbs" — downloadable certificates for heavy metals,
pesticides, identity, potency, keyed to the bottle ID), plus ISO-17025 in-house lab accreditation and
disclosed valerenic acid. It's a US import (no AUST L, no TGA oversight), but on actual
content-verification and transparency it beats every AU-listed rival.

## The "used in real trials" case: Flordis ReDormin Forte

Built on the named **Ze 91019** valerian-hops extract, which has 5 primary RCTs — one (Schicktanz
2025, PMID 40462685, NCT05684523) names "Redormin 500", i.e. THIS exact product. So its "clinically
researched" marketing is genuinely backed (caveat: trials are mostly Max Zeller-sponsored). This is a
real **used-in-published-trials display flag**. Contrast **BioCeuticals**, whose "clinically trialled"
claim borrows a trial from a *different* phyto-equivalent extract (LI-156/Sedonium), not its own — a
marketing-honesty flag to weigh.

## Safety theme to surface (the valerian version of the B6 note)

The TGA has an ingredient-level **valerian liver-injury (hepatotoxicity) advisory** (15 Oct 2020) and
mandates an on-pack liver warning on AU valerian products. Milder than magnesium's B6 story, but a
genuine safety flag worth a page-level note. Note: the **imports (NOW, Gaia) do NOT carry the
AU-mandated valerian warning** — NOW's label has a US Prop-65 lead warning instead. So an Australian
importing them misses the local safety warning: a real consumer signal.

## Regulatory record (all benign so far)

No efficacy/claims enforcement names any valerian product here. What exists is benign: a TGA
**warning-statement compliance sweep** targeted several products (Thompson's, Swisse, Nature's Own,
Caruso's) — all **passed** ("continues to be permitted", no safety issue). Caruso's has an expired
label-layout Section-14 consent (CON-845) and MediHerb a label-presentation consent (CON-993) — both
cosmetic. **Caruso's real enforcement (the $82,500 magnesium infringement, a tampering recall of Wee
Less / Bloat Eze / Ashwagandha 7500) does NOT name valerian** — held as other-product context.

## One rubric question to settle BEFORE scoring (owner)

**Unstandardised whole-root / non-disclosure of valerenic acid** — does it affect a SOURCING score,
or is potency an evidence/efficacy matter? Recommend, mirroring the magnesium oxide ruling:
- **Non-disclosure of valerenic acid → a TRANSPARENCY matter** (the brand isn't telling you the active
  content). Affects Nature's Own (both SKUs), Blackmores Deep Sleep, NOW.
- **Whole-root vs standardised extract (potency itself) → an evidence/efficacy matter (the remedy
  grade), NOT a sourcing penalty.** But an efficacy/"standardised"/"clinically" MARKETING claim not
  backed for that product IS a marketing-honesty issue (e.g. BioCeuticals' borrowed trial).

## Data gaps / corrections (systemic)

- **Sponsor corrections the agents caught:** Thompson's = Homart Wellness (acquired 2024, not Integria);
  Flordis ReDormin Forte ARTG sponsor of record = Actor Pharmaceuticals (not Flordis/SFI).
- Several **AUST L numbers confirmed live on the ARTG** but the on-pack "AUST L nnnnn" string still
