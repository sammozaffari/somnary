## Standardisation & dose transparency (valerian-specific)

Exactly what the label discloses (verbatim, manufacturer FORMULATION block,
https://carusosnaturalhealth.com.au/products/valerian, and Chemist Warehouse
active-ingredients panel, both captured 2026-07-20):

> "Each tablet contains:
> Valeriana officinalis (Valerian) ext. dry conc. 562.50mg
> From min. dry root 2.25g (2,250mg)
> std. to Valerenic acid 4.5mg"

- **Valerenic acid disclosed:** **YES — 4.5 mg/tablet**, an absolute mg figure
  (not a %). This is the strongest standardisation signal on the valerian axis and
  the **highest disclosed valerenic-acid figure in the single-herb pilot set**.
- **Extract vs root powder:** an **extract** ("ext. dry conc."), not raw root powder.
- **Extract ratio / dry-root equivalent:** **disclosed** — 562.5 mg dry-conc
  extract "From min. dry root 2.25g (2,250mg)" (≈4:1). Both numbers on-label.
- **Valerian dose per serving:** disclosed and unambiguous (single-herb, so not
  buried in a combo total).
- **Minor wording variance (recorded, not scored):** the manufacturer FORMULATION
  block and the CW active panel print "**Valerenic acid**" (singular); a review
  aggregator and one retailer paraphrase render it "**Valerenic acids**" (plural,
  the class incl. acetoxy-/hydroxy-valerenic acid). The primary label text read
  on 2026-07-20 says "Valerenic acid 4.5mg" (singular). Recorded verbatim.
- **CW "Standardised herbal extract" key-feature line** (verbatim, CW, 2026-07-20):
  the CW listing adds "Standardised herbal extract" and "Convenient 1 a day
  tablet" to its KEY FEATURES bullet list — a standardisation claim made in
  marketing copy, consistent with the disclosed 4.5 mg figure.
