## Liver-safety relevance (→ page-level safety note; NOT a rubric dimension — DOUBLE-FLAG)

> This section exists because ashwagandha carries a **page-level liver-safety note**
> (the ashwagandha analogue of valerian's safety note). **Two independent risk
> amplifiers stack on this specific product** and must both be flagged. Recorded as
> factual context for the safety module; **not scored.**

**1. Plant part — root+leaf is the higher-scrutiny form (and this product's part is
CONFLICTED toward root+leaf).**
- The **TGA safety advisory "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare risk of
  liver injury" (Feb 2024)** warns of rare hepatotoxicity (and more commonly severe
  GI symptoms), with some AU cases requiring hospitalisation, and the TGA is
  explicitly **weighing root vs leaf** — **India permits root use and has restricted
  leaf use**; **leaf-containing preparations draw higher regulatory scrutiny.**
  Corroborated by **Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (PMID 31991029)** —
  a 5-case Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury — and by the
  NIH LiverTox monograph.
- This product's plant part is **documented-conflicting**: iHerb/Amazon/Walmart
  marketing says **"root and leaf"**; three retailer panels render **"(Root)."**
  Per brief it is **treated as ROOT+LEAF**, i.e. the **higher-scrutiny** form — so
  the liver-safety note should apply on the conservative reading **until a human
  confirms the on-pack ingredient line.** **First flag.**

**2. Import with NO AUST L — NO mandated AU liver warning reaches the consumer.**
- Because this is a **personal-import supplement with no AUST L**, it sits **outside
  TGA medicine oversight** and **carries none of the AU labelling the TGA advisory
  may attach to listed ashwagandha medicines.** A consumer buying it via iHerb AU
  gets the **US label**, which prints no AU liver-warning statement. So the
  higher-scrutiny (root+leaf) form is delivered **without the domestic warning
  scaffolding** that a TGA-listed product would carry. **Second flag.**

**Net (recorded, not scored):** root+leaf (higher-scrutiny) **AND** import-with-no-
AU-warning **stack on the same product** — the double-flag the brief calls for. The
safety module for the ashwagandha remedy page (avoid-in-pregnancy/breastfeeding per
NCCIH; rare hepatotoxicity per TGA/LiverTox; root-vs-leaf caveat) should treat this
SKU as a case where **both** amplifiers apply. **Route the TGA advisory and
Björnsson (PMID 31991029) through the citation-auditor against live primary pages
before publication** (TGA pages repeatedly time out / 403 on automated fetch).
