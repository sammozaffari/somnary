## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per TGA: up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had received **12 reports** of liver
  problems in consumers taking Withania-containing products; **7** had enough
  information to suggest Withania-associated liver injury, and in **4** of those no
  other ingredient was a likely contributor; **most recovered on stopping**, some
  required treatment, and **4 cases required hospitalisation**. (These figures came
  via TGA-page summary / search on 2026-07-20 because tga.gov.au advisory pages
  timed out / were read via summaries — **route through the citation-auditor against
  the live primary TGA advisory before publication**, per the _universe.md note.)
- **Root vs leaf:** the TGA notes **India banned Withania somnifera leaf use** while
  **root use remains permitted subject to limits**, and the TGA is weighing the role
  of different plant parts. **This product is ROOT-ONLY (KSM-66)** — i.e. the
  **lower-risk plant part** in that root-vs-leaf framing. Recorded as a factual
  safety-relevant attribute (root-only), NOT as a safety endorsement.
- **International corroboration (from _universe.md, not re-verified here):**
  Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case
  Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to
  ashwagandha; plus the NIH LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and
  breastfeeding** (NCCIH). This product's label says to "seek the advice of a
  healthcare professional before taking this product" if pregnant/breastfeeding
  (not an outright "do not use").
- **Label gap noted:** as recorded under Transparency, the manufacturer/CW pages
  read on 2026-07-20 did **not** show an explicit hepatotoxicity/liver-injury
  warning statement. A human should confirm the current physical-pack label and the
  ARTG record for any mandated Withania liver-warning statement (the TGA has been
  considering warning-statement requirements for Withania products).
