# Ashwagandha candidate product universe (AU-first) — `[HUMAN-GATE]`

**Status:** candidate list for owner review; human-gated so selection can't be accused of
cherry-picking. Sourced by a research agent (2026-07-20), trimmed to a 12-product pilot. No product
here is scored, ranked, or judged — this is only *which products we research*.

## Inclusion criteria, applied evenly

- **Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*), taken for stress/sleep, is the lead ingredient.** Single-herb
  prioritised; a bounded set of common AU ashwagandha-forward stress/sleep combos admitted, each
  labeled `combo`. The stress/sleep trials use standardised extracts at **300–600 mg/day of root
  extract standardised to withanolides** — that is the reference point.
- **STANDARDISATION to WITHANOLIDE % + whether it is a NAMED CLINICALLY-STUDIED EXTRACT is the quality
  axis** (ashwagandha's analogue of valerenic acid for valerian, or "form" for magnesium). Captured for
  every product: withanolide %/mg per dose; root-only vs root+leaf; **named extract — KSM-66** (Ixoreal,
  root-only, ~5% withanolides), **Sensoril** (Natreon, root+leaf, ~10%), **Shoden** (root+leaf,
  standardised 35% withanolide glycosides) — vs **generic/unstated**; extract ratio / dry-root
  equivalent; mg/serving. A product using a named trial extract carries a "used in published trials"
  signal (like Flordis Ze 91019 for valerian). Non-disclosure is recorded, not skipped.
- **The AUST L "listed medicine" vs "food" distinction is captured for every product.** Unlike glycine
  (mostly sold as foods), **most AU ashwagandha IS a TGA-listed complementary medicine with an AUST L**
  — ashwagandha is not an approved general food ingredient in AU, so the domestic products carry an
  ARTG listing and TGA label/claim oversight. iHerb imports carry **no AUST L** (personal importation).
  This split is a first-class signal, not a footnote.
- **Availability:** Chemist Warehouse / Amazon AU / Woolworths (AU brands), practitioner channels, and
  iHerb AU (imports). AUST L captured where the product is a listed medicine.
- **Quality spectrum evenly:** mainstream AU chemist, practitioner-tier AU, named-extract
  (KSM-66/Shoden) products, and an iHerb-imported contrast — reached deliberately so the set is not
  stacked toward the standardised/named-extract end.

## Notes for the owner

- **The standardisation + named-extract axis (ashwagandha's analogue of valerenic acid):** the pilot is
  built to contrast **named trial extract vs generic**. Five products name **KSM-66** (Caruso's 7500,
  both Nature's Way, Switch, Herbs of Gold Mind Ease, Fusion) — root-only, ~5% withanolides, the AU
  default. One names **Shoden** (Green Nutritionals, 35% withanolide glycosides, root+leaf) — the
  highest-standardisation extract in the set. The rest are **generic/unstated** (Blackmores ~0.3%,
  Swisse 1.5 mg, Thompson's 3.75 mg, Gaia proprietary blend, NOW min 2.5%). This is the cleanest
  standardisation contrast in the set, directly parallel to valerian's valerenic-acid disclosure gap.
- **"Used in published trials" flag (KSM-66/Shoden):** KSM-66 and Shoden both have RCTs, so a product
  using them carries a factual "trial-studied extract" signal — the ashwagandha analogue of Flordis
  Ze 91019 for valerian and Thorne's NSF cert for magnesium. Flag at scoring whether "named
  clinically-studied extract" is a distinct higher signal (rubric already treats *"used in published
  trials"* as **context, not credit** — trial use is not a quality endorsement; keep that tooltip discipline).
- **The liver-safety signal — a page-level safety note will be needed (like valerian's).** The TGA
  published a safety advisory, **"Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare risk of liver injury"
  (Feb 2024)**, warning of rare hepatotoxicity (and more commonly severe GI symptoms); some AU cases
  required hospitalisation. Corroborated internationally by **Björnsson et al., *Liver International*
  2020 (PMID 31991029)** — a 5-case Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury — and by
  2023 Iceland/Denmark consumer warnings and the NIH LiverTox monograph. TGA is weighing **root vs leaf**
  (India banned leaf use; root permitted with limits) — which makes the root-only vs root+leaf capture on
  every card safety-relevant, not just a quality axis. **Pregnancy:** the standard reference position is
  **avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding** (NCCIH, verbatim). Exact TGA case/hospitalisation counts and
  the "~320 ARTG listings" figure came via search summaries (TGA/ODS pages repeatedly timed out / 403'd on
  automated fetch) — **route through the citation-auditor against the live primary TGA pages before publication.**
- **The Caruso's Ashwagandha 7500 recall — directly on-topic, flag for scoring.** TGA recall
  **10 December 2024**: Caruso's Ashwagandha 7500 (batch **Q01687**, exp Sep 2027) recalled alongside
  Caruso's Wee Less and Bloat Eze for **suspected tampering** — unknown capsules differing in appearance
  from the product's own tablets (preliminary indication of cocoa powder + probiotics), composition/risk
  undetermined; consumers told to return for refund. TGA later **cancelled** a Caruso's Ashwagandha
  listing under s30(1)(c). Unlike valerian (where the tampering recall was held as an other-product
  event), this recall names the ashwagandha product itself — it attaches to the responsible sponsor
  (Caruso's Natural Health Pty Ltd) under the rubric's Regulatory-record dimension. Capture the primary
  recall + cancellation URLs; quote the TGA wording verbatim, dated, no adjectives (defamation guardrail).
- **AUST-L-medicine-vs-food split (capture per-product):** the reverse of glycine — **the AU ashwagandha
  a consumer takes for stress/sleep is almost always a TGA-listed medicine WITH an AUST L** (Caruso's
  406701, Nature's Way 384294, Switch 444675, Herbs of Gold 323152, Blackmores 310894, Swisse 390319,
  Thompson's 430133 all captured), while the **iHerb imports (Gaia, NOW) carry no AUST L** and sit outside
  TGA oversight. So domestic ashwagandha has more label/claim oversight than domestic glycine did — a
  structural point the scorecard should make legible, and one the recall complicates (an AUST L did not
  prevent the tampering event).
- **Capture gaps to close before scoring:** confirm Nature's Way Sound Sleep AUST L (508647) on the ARTG;
  Green Nutritionals Shoden per-capsule mg (label-image only, contact M&P); Fusion Stress & Anxiety and
  Switch withanolide %; Caruso's second circulating ARTG number (308970 vs 406701); Gaia/NOW withanolide
  figures against the brands' own panels (some came from third-party listings); the Sensoril AU-SKU search;
  and community-read themes gathered from public forums by a human (vendor star-reviews excluded by README rule).
- **Rubric question to settle at scoring (like valerian's unstandardised whole-root ruling):** does an
  **unstandardised / generic / non-disclosed-withanolide** product incur a SOURCING penalty, or is potency
  an evidence/efficacy matter? Recommend mirroring the valerian/magnesium rulings: **non-disclosure →
  Transparency; potency/standardisation of the form → evidence grade, not a sourcing penalty.** The named
  trial extract (KSM-66/Shoden) reads as an unscored *"used in published trials"* context flag, not credit.
