> Folic acid 150 micrograms

> Zinc amino acid chelate 30 mg (equiv. zinc 6 mg)"

  (ashwagandha) extract dry conc." — a **generic** dry-concentrate extract. This is

- **Root vs root+leaf:** **ROOT ONLY** — "equiv. to dry root 1.5 g"; no leaf.

## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **This product's label CARRIES a liver-caution statement (PRIMARY, verbatim,
  2026-07-20):** > "Do not use during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or if you have
  pre-existing liver condition." This is a **liver-specific contraindication on-label**
  — a point of contrast with the Caruso's KSM-66 dossier, whose captured pages showed
  pregnancy/medication warnings but **no** explicit liver statement. (Recorded as a
  factual label attribute, **not** a safety endorsement.)
- **Root vs leaf:** **ROOT ONLY** ("equiv. to dry root 1.5 g") — the **lower-risk
  plant part** in the TGA's root-vs-leaf framing (India banned Withania **leaf** use;
  root permitted subject to limits). Recorded as a factual safety-relevant attribute.
- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per TGA (figures via TGA-scoped search summary on 2026-07-20 because the
  advisory page timed out on automated WebFetch): up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had
  received **12 reports** of liver problems in consumers taking Withania-containing
  products; **7** had enough information to suggest Withania-associated liver injury,
  and in **4** of those no other ingredient was a likely contributor; **most
  recovered on stopping**, some required treatment, and **4 cases required
  hospitalisation.** Since the 2024 advisory the TGA reported **a further 4** adverse
  event reports suggesting possible Withania-related liver injury, **all resolved on
  discontinuation, none requiring hospitalisation.** (**Route through the
  citation-auditor against the live primary TGA advisory before publication.**)
- **Related TGA safety-alert pages (primary, for the auditor to confirm live):**
  https://www.tga.gov.au/safety/safety-monitoring-and-information/safety-alerts/medicines-containing-withania-somnifera-withania-ashwagandha
  and .../herbal-preparations-containing-withania-somnifera-withania-ashwagandha
- **International corroboration (from `_universe.md`, not re-verified here):**
  Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case
  Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to
  ashwagandha; plus the NIH LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and
  breastfeeding** (NCCIH). This product's label goes further than Caruso's — an
  outright > "Do not use during pregnancy or breastfeeding" — rather than a
  "seek advice" statement.
