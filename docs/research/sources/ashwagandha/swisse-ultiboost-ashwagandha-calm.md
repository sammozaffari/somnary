## Withanolide standardisation & named extract (ashwagandha-specific: withanolide % + named extract)

Exactly what the label discloses (verbatim; Swisse product page and Chemist
Warehouse active-ingredients panel, both captured 2026-07-21). The two sources
agree on the active panel:

> "Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera extract 30 mg) equiv. dry root 1.5 g,
> standardised to contain withanolides of withania somnifera 1.5 mg
> Passionflower (Passiflora incarnata extract 60 mg) equiv. dry herb 3 g
> Reishi mushroom (Ganoderma lucidum extract 50 mg) equiv. dry fruiting body 1 g
> Magnesium (from magnesium citrate) 52.5 mg"

- **Withanolides disclosed:** **YES — 1.5 mg / tablet**, an absolute mg figure.
  Relative to the 30 mg extract this is **1.5 / 30 = 5.0% of the extract** — i.e. the
  extract itself is a ~5%-withanolide extract, comparable in % to KSM-66. **But the
  absolute delivery is tiny** because the extract weight is only 30 mg: **1.5 mg
  withanolides/tablet, 3 mg/day** at the 2-tablet dose. This is the **lowest absolute
  withanolide figure in the entire pilot set** on both a per-tablet and a per-day
  basis (cf. Caruso's 30 mg/tablet KSM-66, ~60 mg/day; Blackmores 4.5 mg/tablet,
  9 mg/day; Thompson's 3.75 mg/tablet). The **% looks respectable, the milligram
  delivery does not** — capture both so the low absolute dose is not masked by the
  ~5% figure. **This is the defining feature of the SKU: a LOW-dose, generic
  ashwagandha.**
- **Named extract disclosed:** **NO.** No KSM-66 / Sensoril / Shoden or any other
  named clinically-studied extract appears on the label or in marketing on the pages
  read (2026-07-21). The extract is described only as "*Withania somnifera* extract"
  — a **generic** dry-concentrate extract. Alongside Blackmores and Thompson's, this
  is the cleanest "generic vs named-extract" contrast in the set. **On-brand contrast
  (context, not this SKU):** Swisse's separate "High Strength Ashwagandha" DOES use
  KSM-66 — so within one brand's range, the *named-extract* product and this
  *generic* Calm+ product sit side by side.
- **Root vs root+leaf:** **ROOT ONLY** — "equiv. dry root 1.5 g"; no leaf.
  (Safety-relevant — see Liver-safety relevance.)
- **Extract ratio / dry-root equivalent:** **disclosed** — 30 mg extract "equiv. dry
  root 1.5 g (1500 mg)" (~50:1). Both numbers on-label. (Note: same 1.5 g dry-root
  equivalent as Blackmores, but from only 30 mg extract vs Blackmores' 150 mg — a
  higher nominal concentration ratio, but a far smaller extract mass.)
- **Ashwagandha dose per serving:** 2 tablets/day = **60 mg generic extract/day**
  (≈ 3 g dry-root eq/day). The **60 mg extract/day is FAR BELOW the 300–600 mg/day**
  of standardised extract used by the stress/sleep RCTs cited in `_universe.md`. Even
  the dry-root-equivalent basis (~3 g/day) sits below those trials' extract potency
  because the trials used **named, higher-withanolide-delivery extracts** (KSM-66 at
  600 mg/day delivers ~30 mg withanolides; this product delivers **3 mg/day**).
  Recorded as a factual dose/standardisation gap; **not scored, not an efficacy
  judgment.** (The magnesium at 52.5 mg/tablet / 105 mg/day carries its own AU-listed
  "supports a healthy stress response" indication — see Marketing claims — which may
  be doing more of the label's stress messaging than the sub-trial-dose ashwagandha.)

## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **Root vs leaf:** **ROOT ONLY** ("equiv. dry root 1.5 g") — the **lower-risk plant
  part** in the TGA's root-vs-leaf framing (India banned Withania **leaf** use; root
  permitted subject to limits). Recorded as a factual safety-relevant attribute, NOT a
  safety endorsement.
- **Low dose is itself safety-relevant context (recorded, not scored):** at **1.5 mg
  withanolides/tablet (3 mg/day)** and **60 mg extract/day**, the ashwagandha exposure
  here is **far below** the 300–600 mg/day standardised-extract trial range — i.e. the
  lowest ashwagandha exposure in the pilot. The DILIN/TGA hepatotoxicity case reports
  are idiosyncratic (not clearly dose-linear), so a low dose is **not** a guarantee of
  safety; recorded as factual exposure context, not a risk clearance.
- **Label liver-statement gap noted:** the Swisse/CW pages read on 2026-07-21 carried
  a general pregnancy/breastfeeding "consult a professional" line but **no explicit
  hepatotoxicity/liver-injury warning statement**. A human should confirm the current
  physical-pack label and the ARTG record for any mandated Withania liver-warning
  statement (the TGA Feb-2024 advisory and ongoing warning-statement consideration
  make this material).
- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per TGA (figures via TGA-scoped search summary on 2026-07-21 because the
  advisory page timed out on automated WebFetch): up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had received
  **12 reports** of liver problems in consumers taking Withania-containing products;
  **7** had enough information to suggest Withania-associated liver injury, and in **4**
  of those no other ingredient was a likely contributor; **most recovered on
  stopping**, some required treatment, and **4 cases required hospitalisation.**
  (**Route through the citation-auditor against the live primary TGA advisory before
  publication.**)
- **International corroboration (from `_universe.md`, not re-verified here):**
  Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case
  Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to
  ashwagandha; plus the NIH LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and
