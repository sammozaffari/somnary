## Standardisation & dose transparency (ashwagandha-specific: withanolide % + named extract)

Active panel as published across retailer listings (captured 2026-07-20):

> Shoden® [extract] 240 mg

- **Withanolide standardisation disclosed:** **YES — 35% withanolide glycosides**, the **highest standardisation figure in the pilot set**, and **84 mg withanolide glycosides/capsule** as an absolute mg. **Important caveat (record, do not score):** Shoden's "**withanolide glycosides**" assay is a **different analytical basis** from KSM-66's "**withanolides** (HPLC)". - **Named extract disclosed:** **YES — "Shoden®"** named explicitly on the label and in marketing copy. Shoden® is a next-generation *Withania somnifera* **root + leaf** extract standardised to 35% withanolide glycosides.
- **Root vs root+leaf:** **ROOT + LEAF.** Shoden® is manufactured from **ashwagandha root AND leaf** (confirmed against the Shoden clinical literature, which describes "ashwagandha root and leaf extract ... 35% withanolide glycosides (Shoden)"). Retail panels phrase the botanical source as "*Withania somnifera* (Ashwagandha) whole plant" equivalent. **This is the higher-scrutiny plant-part form — see Liver-safety relevance.**
- **Extract / whole-plant equivalent:** disclosed as "whole plant (min) 9.12 g" (most retailers) or "9.36 g" (one retailer) per 240 mg extract. Two figures circulate — a capture gap to resolve on the physical bottle.
- **Ashwagandha dose per serving:** 1 capsule/day = **240 mg Shoden® extract / 84 mg withanolide glycosides**. **Note on dose vs the Shoden trials:** the published Shoden sleep/stress RCTs dosed **120 mg/day of Shoden** (2 × 60 mg capsules, ~42 mg withanolide glycosides/day). **This product delivers 240 mg Shoden/day — double the trial dose** on a per-mg-extract basis. ## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. **This product is a ROOT + LEAF extract — the higher-scrutiny
> plant-part form.** Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **ROOT + LEAF = the higher-scrutiny form.** Shoden® is manufactured from ashwagandha **root AND leaf**. In the TGA's root-vs-leaf framing, **leaf-containing** products sit on the higher-scrutiny side: the TGA notes **India banned *Withania somnifera* leaf use** while **root use remains permitted subject to limits**, and the TGA is weighing the role of different plant parts. Unlike the KSM-66 products in the set (root-only), **this product contains leaf material** — a factual safety-relevant attribute, recorded as such (NOT a safety verdict).
- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare risk of liver injury" (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury, Feb 2024) — rare hepatotoxicity signal (and more commonly severe GI symptoms); some AU cases required hospitalisation. (Case figures reached via TGA-page summary because tga.gov.au advisory pages timed out on automated fetch — **route through the citation-auditor against the live primary TGA advisory before publication**, per the _universe.md note.)
- **International corroboration (from _universe.md, not re-verified here):** Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to ashwagandha; plus the NIH LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding** (NCCIH). **This product carries a pregnancy warning (verbatim, Pharmacy Online panel, 2026-07-20):**
  > "If symptoms persist, talk to your health professional. If you are pregnant, or considering becoming pregnant, do not take without consulting a health professional."
- **Label gap noted:** the manufacturer/retailer pages read on 2026-07-20 did **not** show an explicit **hepatotoxicity / liver-injury** warning statement (only the pregnancy/"symptoms persist" statements above). Given this is a **root+leaf** (higher-scrutiny) product, a human should confirm the current physical-pack label and the ARTG record for any mandated Withania liver-warning statement — this is **more** material here than for the root-only KSM-66 products in the set.
