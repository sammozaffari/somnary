## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per the _universe.md capture: up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had received
  **12 reports** of liver problems in consumers taking Withania-containing products;
  **7** were suggestive of Withania-associated liver injury, **4** with no other
  likely contributor; most recovered on stopping; **4 cases required
  hospitalisation.** (These figures came via TGA-page summary because tga.gov.au
  pages time out on automated fetch — **route through the citation-auditor against
  the live primary TGA advisory before publication**, per the _universe.md note.)
- **Root vs leaf:** the TGA notes **India banned Withania somnifera leaf use** while
  **root use remains permitted subject to limits**, and is weighing the role of
  different plant parts. **This product is ROOT-ONLY (KSM-66)** — the **lower-risk
  plant part** in that root-vs-leaf framing. Recorded as a factual safety-relevant
  attribute (root-only), NOT as a safety endorsement. (The lavender component is a
  flower extract, unrelated to the Withania liver signal.)
- **International corroboration (from _universe.md, not re-verified here):**
  Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case
  Iceland/US DILIN series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to
  ashwagandha; plus the NIH LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and
  breastfeeding** (NCCIH). The captured web copy for Mind Ease did **not** show a
  pregnancy/breastfeeding statement — confirm the on-bottle warning panel.
- **Label gap noted:** the manufacturer/retailer pages read on 2026-07-20 did **not**
  show an explicit hepatotoxicity/liver-injury warning statement (nor a pregnancy
  statement). A human should confirm the current physical-pack label and the ARTG
  323152 record for any mandated Withania liver-warning statement (the TGA has been
  considering warning-statement requirements for Withania products).
