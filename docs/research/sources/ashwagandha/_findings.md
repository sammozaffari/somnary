# Ashwagandha dossiers — consolidation & scoring inputs (AU pilot)

Research complete for all 12 candidates (2026-07-20/21). **Gather-only: no scores drafted here.** Read
before the scoring pass. Owner greenlit the 12-product universe and ruled the Caruso's 7500 recall =
a regulatory DEDUCTION with the tampering context shown verbatim (it was suspected third-party
tampering, not a Caruso's manufacturing defect).

## The central axis: withanolide standardisation + named clinical extract
Like valerenic acid for valerian. The set splits into three tiers:
- **KSM-66 (6):** Caruso's 7500, Nature's Way Sound Sleep, Nature's Way 6000, Switch Nutrition, Herbs
  of Gold Mind Ease (combo), Fusion Health (combo). Root-only, ~5% withanolides, the AU default.
- **Shoden (1):** Green Nutritionals — root+LEAF, standardised 35% withanolide glycosides (highest
  figure in the set, but a DIFFERENT assay basis than KSM-66's ~5% HPLC withanolides — NOT a like-for-
  like potency ranking; don't present it as "strongest").
- **Generic / unnamed (5):** Blackmores Ashwagandha+ (~4.5 mg), Swisse Calm+ (1.5 mg — the lowest),
  Thompson's (3.75 mg/tablet), Gaia (2.5 mg proprietary blend — NOT KSM-66), NOW (min 2.5% / ~11 mg,
  root+leaf).

## The disclosure gap (the "Forte 2000 test" for ashwagandha)
Most products DISCLOSE withanolide mg (Caruso's 30, NW6000 15, Switch 15, Herbs of Gold 15, Green
Nutritionals 84 glycosides, Thompson's 3.75, Swisse 1.5, Blackmores ~4.5, Gaia 2.5, NOW ~11). The two
NON-disclosers are **Nature's Way Sound Sleep** and **Fusion Health** — both KSM-66, both omit the
withanolide figure their KSM-66 siblings print. Sound Sleep is the sharpest: same brand as NW6000
(which discloses 15 mg), same KSM-66, but no figure. → Transparency, per the valerian ruling.

## "Used in published trials" — context flag, NOT credit (rubric discipline), + three claim mismatches
KSM-66 and Shoden both have RCTs, so products using them carry a factual "trial-studied extract" flag
(like Flordis Ze 91019 for valerian). Rubric treats it as context, not a quality endorsement. THREE
marketing-claim mismatches surfaced (weigh at scoring):
- **Herbs of Gold Mind Ease** footnotes Chandrasekhar 2012 (a real KSM-66 RCT) as "clinically trialled",
  but that trial dosed **600 mg/day KSM-66 monotherapy** while one Mind Ease tablet delivers **300 mg**
  (half) in an ashwagandha+lavender combo — a dose/scope mismatch (the borrowed-trial-at-wrong-dose
  pattern, cf. BioCeuticals on valerian). Candidate marketing −1 — RATIFY.
- **Green Nutritionals Shoden** "clinically studied" — delivers 240 mg/day vs the Shoden trials' 120 mg
  (DOUBLE, i.e. meets/exceeds the trial dose) → the claim is supported, no deduction.
- **Fusion Health** headline clinical claim rides on **OciBest holy basil** (its own RCT at a whole-
  formula-equivalent dose), NOT the KSM-66 — so the KSM-66 trial isn't the borrowed thing; a retailer
  (not the brand) titles it "clinically-proven". No deduction on the brand copy.
No "clinically studied" claim was found for Caruso's, Sound Sleep, NW6000, Switch, Swisse, Thompson's,
Blackmores, Gaia, or NOW (recorded as absence).

## The Caruso's 7500 recall — the one on-product regulatory event (deduct + tampering context)
Two DISTINCT records, kept precise:
- **ARTG 406701 "Ashwagandha 7500" is ACTIVE.** The TGA recall (10 Dec 2024, batch **Q01687**, exp Sep
  2027) was a **batch-specific recall for SUSPECTED THIRD-PARTY TAMPERING** — unknown inserted capsules
  differing from the product's own tablets (preliminary indication cocoa powder + probiotics),
  composition "not yet been determined"; consumers told to return for refund. Explicitly NOT a Caruso's
  manufacturing/quality defect.
- **ARTG 308970 "Caruso's Ashwagandha" was CANCELLED** under s30(1)(c), date of effect 28 June 2024 (a
  separate, older listing; the cancellation page states section + date, no reason — infer nothing).
Owner ruling: this attaches to the Regulatory dimension as a deduction, WITH the tampering context
stated verbatim. Recommend regulatory ≈ 3 (a real recall + cancellation naming the product, mitigated
because the recall cause was suspected external tampering, not a brand quality defect) — RATIFY the exact number.

## Regulatory — everything else off-product (held, not deducted)
- **Switch Nutrition:** TGA $79,200 infringement notices (30 Apr 2026) for **NMN + Vitamin D3** advertising
  — not the ashwagandha product. Held (same-sponsor, other-product), like Caruso's magnesium fines were on valerian.
- **PharmaCare** (Nature's Way parent): 2020 $164,520 Vita Gummies milk-allergen fine + Vita Gummies/probiotic
  cancellations — other products. Held. (Do NOT conflate AU PharmaCare with the historic US "Nature's Way Products, Inc.")
- **Herbs of Gold:** s30(1)(c) cancellations on CoQ10/fish oil — other products. Held.
- **Swisse:** two sibling "Calm + De-Stress" SKU cancellations (s30(1)(c)) — other products, no recall. Held.
- **Thompson's:** ARTG 377116 cancelled s30(1)(c) 28 Jun 2024 under FORMER sponsor Integria — an ownership-
  transfer retirement (Homart acquired Thompson's 2024), not compliance; the July-2025 SpringLeaf recall is a
  different entity (Homart Pharmaceuticals). Held.
- **Fusion / Global Therapeutics:** Tienchi Ginseng compliance review (no safety issue) + Libido lab PASS +
  s30(1)(c) cancellations — other products. Held.
- **NOW / Gaia (imports):** NOW 2004 ginseng WL + Salmonella food recalls (other products); Gaia's 2020 COVID
  warning letter was a DIFFERENT entity ("Gaia's Whole Healing Essentials, LLC", not Gaia Herbs, Inc.). Held.

## Testing & verification — Gaia is the standout (as for valerian)
No product holds a finished-product 3rd-party cert (USP/NSF/Informed) or a published product-naming assay.
KSM-66/Shoden are INGREDIENT pedigrees (raw-material COAs from Ixoreal/Arjuna), NOT finished-product certs.
**Gaia Herbs** is the only product publishing **per-batch "Meet Your Herbs" lab reports** (identity +
contaminants + marker compounds, keyed to the bottle batch, ISO-17025 in-house lab) covering this SKU — so
Gaia earns testing/label credit (parallel to Gaia valerian: ~2/2), the verification standout, despite being a
low-withanolide proprietary blend and an import.

## Additives — mostly clean / disclosed-or-provisional
Single-herb caps + disclosed panels (NOW: hypromellose/rice flour/mag stearate — clean; Gaia: glycerin etc.)
→ firm 5. AU combos with unpublished full panels (Blackmores, Swisse, Thompson's, Herbs of Gold, Fusion,
Caruso's "contains lactose" only) → provisional 5. No confirmed additive-of-concern anywhere.

## Liver safety — a page-level note needed (like valerian's)
TGA advisory "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare risk of liver injury" (Feb 2024); Björnsson 2020
(PMID 31991029). ROOT vs LEAF is safety-relevant (India banned leaf use; root permitted with limits):
- **Root+LEAF (higher scrutiny):** Green Nutritionals (Shoden), NOW (root+leaf per marketing; some panels say
  root — flagged). Flag these specifically.
- **Imports skip the AU liver warning:** Gaia, NOW carry no AU-mandated advisory.
- **Pregnancy:** avoid (NCCIH) — several labels carry the pregnancy warning.

## AUST-L medicine vs food — the reverse of glycine
Most AU ashwagandha IS a TGA-listed medicine (AUST L captured: Caruso's 406701, NW6000 384294, Switch 444675,
Herbs of Gold 323152, Blackmores 310894, Swisse 390319, Thompson's 430133, Green Nutritionals 507011, Fusion
353060 [retailer-asserted], Sound Sleep 508647 [retailer-asserted]). Imports (Gaia, NOW) carry no AUST L. So
domestic ashwagandha has more label/claim oversight than domestic glycine — yet an AUST L did NOT prevent the
Caruso's tampering event (a point worth surfacing).
