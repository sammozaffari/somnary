## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific safety note)

Recorded as a **page-level safety context note** (the ashwagandha analogue of the
valerian liver advisory), NOT a finding against this product and NOT scored:
- **Root-only = the lower-risk form in the current regulatory framing.** Sound Sleep
  uses **KSM-66, a ROOT-ONLY extract** ("root ext." on-label). The TGA safety update
  and international reviews flag that regulators are weighing **root vs leaf** — India
  **banned leaf use** while **root remains permitted subject to limits**
  (`_universe.md`; nutraingredients 2024-02-26 coverage). A root-only product sits on
  the **lower-risk side** of that root-vs-leaf distinction. Recorded as a factual
  form attribute, not a safety endorsement.
- **BUT the ingredient-class hepatotoxicity signal still applies.** The TGA Feb-2024
  advisory (12 reports / 7 probable / 4 hospitalisations up to 5 Feb 2024, per search
  summaries) concerns *W. somnifera* generally, root included; corroborated
  internationally by **Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (PMID 31991029)**
  (5-case DILIN cholestatic/mixed series) and the NIH LiverTox monograph
  (`_universe.md`). The 600 mg extract ≡ 7.5 g/day dose is at the top of the trial
  band. **Pregnancy/breastfeeding:** standard reference position is **avoid** (NCCIH).
- **No on-label liver warning was observed** on this product's surveyed pages
  (2026-07-20) — recorded as absence, for the human/safety-module decision, not scored.
- Route the exact TGA counts/wording and the root-vs-leaf regulatory status through
  the **citation-auditor against the live primary TGA pages** before any publication
  (the TGA pages timed out on automated fetch this pass).
