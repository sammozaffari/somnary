## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — page-level note needed, like valerian's)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely a
> quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per TGA (via search surface, 2026-07-22): up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had
  received **12 reports** of liver problems in consumers taking Withania-containing
  products; **7** had enough information to suggest Withania-associated liver injury;
  since the 2024 advisory a **further 4** such reports were received. As of ~17 July
  the ARTG listed **91** complementary medicines/supplements containing ashwagandha.
  (TGA advisory pages time out on automated fetch — **route these figures through the
  citation-auditor against the live primary TGA advisory before publication**, per the
  _findings.md note.)
- **Root vs leaf:** the TGA is weighing the role of plant parts (India banned Withania
  **leaf** use; **root** use permitted subject to limits). **This product is ROOT-ONLY
  (KSM-66)** — the lower-risk plant part in that framing. Factual safety-relevant
  attribute, NOT a safety endorsement.
- **International corroboration (from _universe.md/_findings.md, not re-verified here):**
  Björnsson et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**); NIH LiverTox
  monograph. Cite/attribute only.
- **Pregnancy:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding**
  (NCCIH). This product's label goes further than the 7500's soft wording: **"Not
  recommended for use by pregnant or lactating women"** (verbatim).
- **Label gap noted:** as under Transparency, the pages read on 2026-07-22 did **not**
  show an explicit hepatotoxicity/liver-injury warning statement. Confirm the current
  physical-pack label and ARTG record for any mandated Withania liver-warning statement.
