## Liver-safety relevance (ashwagandha-specific — added per brief)

> Ashwagandha carries a documented hepatotoxicity signal, so root-vs-leaf and the
> presence/absence of a liver-warning statement are **safety-relevant**, not merely
> a quality axis. Captured here as primary-source context; **not scored**.

- **TGA safety advisory (primary):** "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare
  risk of liver injury"
  (https://www.tga.gov.au/news/safety-updates/withania-somnifera-ashwagandha-and-very-rare-risk-liver-injury,
  Feb 2024). Per the _universe.md capture: up to 5 Feb 2024 the TGA had received
  **12 reports** of liver problems in consumers taking Withania-containing products;
  **7** had enough information to suggest Withania-associated liver injury, **4** of
  those had no other likely contributor, most recovered on stopping, and **4 cases
  required hospitalisation**. (These figures came via TGA-page summary — **route
  through the citation-auditor against the live primary TGA advisory before
  publication**, per the _universe.md note. Not re-verified in-browser here.)
- **Root vs leaf:** the TGA notes **India banned Withania somnifera leaf use** while
  **root use remains permitted subject to limits**, and is weighing the role of
  different plant parts. **This product is ROOT-ONLY** ("derived from dry root min.
  1.5 g"; no leaf declared) — i.e. the **lower-risk plant part** in that root-vs-leaf
  framing. Recorded as a factual safety-relevant attribute (root-only), NOT as a
  safety endorsement.
- **International corroboration (from _universe.md, not re-verified here):** Björnsson
  et al., *Liver International* 2020 (**PMID 31991029**) — a 5-case Iceland/US DILIN
  series of cholestatic/mixed liver injury attributed to ashwagandha; plus the NIH
  LiverTox monograph. Cite/attribute only; not republished.
- **Pregnancy:** standard reference position is **avoid in pregnancy and
  breastfeeding** (NCCIH). The product's on-label warnings were **not fully captured**
  (the Thompson's Warnings section is a collapsible block that did not render; the CW
  page showed only "KEEP OUT OF REACH OF CHILDREN. Do not use if bottle/or cap seal
  is missing or broken."). A human should capture the full physical-pack warnings
  panel — see Open questions.
- **Label gap noted:** the pages read on 2026-07-21 did **not** show an explicit
  hepatotoxicity/liver-injury warning statement. A human should confirm the current
  physical-pack label and the ARTG record for any mandated Withania liver-warning
  statement (the TGA Feb-2024 advisory and ongoing warning-statement consideration
  make this material). This product is a **combo** (also contains passionflower and
  lavender oil), so any liver-relevant note would sit alongside the passionflower's
  own (lower-signal) profile — recorded for completeness, not scored.
