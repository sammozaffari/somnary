## Liver-safety relevance (→ page-level safety note; NOT a rubric dimension — DOUBLE-FLAG)

> This section exists because ashwagandha carries a **page-level liver-safety note** (the
> ashwagandha analogue of valerian's safety note). **Two risk amplifiers stack on this
> specific product.** Recorded as factual context for the safety module; **not scored.**

**1. Plant part — ROOT + LEAF is the higher-scrutiny form.**
- The **TGA safety advisory "Withania somnifera (Ashwagandha) and very rare risk of liver
  injury" (Feb 2024)** warns of rare hepatotoxicity (and more commonly severe GI symptoms),
  with some AU cases requiring hospitalisation, and the TGA is explicitly **weighing root
  vs leaf** — **India permits root use and has restricted leaf use**; leaf-containing
  preparations draw higher regulatory scrutiny. Corroborated by **Björnsson et al., *Liver
  International* 2020 (PMID 31991029)** and the NIH LiverTox monograph.
- Thorne Ashwagandha is **root + leaf** (stated consistently across sources) — the
  **higher-scrutiny** form, same amplifier as Green Nutritionals (Shoden) and NOW. **First flag.**

**2. Import with NO AUST L — NO mandated AU liver warning reaches the consumer.**
- Because this is a **personal-import supplement with no AUST L**, it sits **outside TGA
  medicine oversight** and carries none of the AU labelling the TGA advisory may attach to
  listed ashwagandha medicines. A consumer buying via iHerb AU gets the **US label**, which
  prints no AU liver-warning statement — so the higher-scrutiny (root+leaf) form is
  delivered **without the domestic warning scaffolding** an AUST L product would carry.
  (Thorne's company-wide TGA *manufacturing* certification does NOT change this — it is not
  an AUST L for the SKU.) **Second flag.**

**Net (recorded, not scored):** root+leaf (higher-scrutiny) **AND** import-with-no-AU-
warning **stack on the same product** — the double-flag pattern the brief calls for
(identical to NOW). The safety module for the ashwagandha remedy page (avoid-in-
pregnancy/breastfeeding per NCCIH; rare hepatotoxicity per TGA/LiverTox; root-vs-leaf
caveat) should treat this SKU as a case where **both** amplifiers apply. **Route the TGA
advisory and Björnsson (PMID 31991029) through the citation-auditor against live primary
pages before publication** (TGA pages time out / 403 on automated fetch).
