# IA, Content Roadmap, And Page Briefs

## IA Problem

Somnary's current structure proves credibility, but it still asks new users to orient themselves through a remedy board. First-time users often arrive with a situation, not a molecule: “I wake up at 3am,” “I am taking 10mg melatonin,” “I bought a sleep blend,” or “Is CBD worth it?”

The IA should route by decision intent first, then expose remedy depth.

## Homepage Above-The-Fold

Objective: reduce time-to-understanding to under 8 seconds.

Recommended first viewport:

- headline: “Check a sleep remedy before you take it.”
- subhead: “Somnary grades natural sleep remedies by human evidence, label reality, and safety boundaries. No affiliate links. No brand money.”
- primary CTA: “Check a remedy”
- secondary CTA: “Start from your sleep problem”
- tertiary CTA: “Read safety red flags”

Decision tiles:

- I take or am considering melatonin.
- I bought a sleep blend.
- I want to fall asleep faster.
- I wake up at night.
- I use medications or have a health condition.
- I am considering this for a child.

## Remedy Page Improvements

Add near the top:

- “Best for”
- “Not for”
- “Biggest risk”
- “Evidence in one sentence”
- “What changes your decision”

Keep existing claims-vs-data tables. They are one of the site's strongest product patterns.

Add a short “decision translation” after each grade:

- S: make this the default pathway before supplements when relevant.
- A: reasonable to discuss or consider for the studied use case.
- B: plausible but modest; expectations should be low.
- C: interesting but uncertain; do not rely on it.
- D: claims outrun evidence; avoid spending meaningful money.
- F: risk, regulatory, or evidence problem is the headline.

## SEO Content Patterns

Use these patterns repeatedly:

- “Does [remedy] actually help sleep?”
- “[Remedy] for [goal]: what human evidence shows”
- “[Remedy] dose for sleep: what was studied vs what is sold”
- “[Remedy] safety: who should avoid it”
- “[Remedy] vs [remedy] for sleep”
- “What to check before buying [product category]”

## Design Notes

The site should stay editorial and restrained. Do not add wellness gradients, dream imagery, or supplement-store visual language. The brand advantage is skeptical calm.

Design principles:

- evidence first, commerce never;
- safety before optimization;
- bottom line before detail;
- source-backed claims visible;
- uncertainty treated as useful information;
- fast routing for tired users.
