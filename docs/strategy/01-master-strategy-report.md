# Somnary Strategy Report

Prepared: 2026-07-05  
Working premise: Somnary should become the trusted evidence layer for natural sleep remedies: not a supplement blog, not a generic AI chatbot, and not an affiliate review site.

## 1. Strategic Diagnosis

Somnary already has a rare asset in the sleep-wellness category: a clear editorial doctrine. The site grades remedies by published human evidence, separates remedy evidence from product QA and community reports, rejects affiliate incentives, and shows weak evidence instead of hiding it. That position is stronger than most content sites in this market.

The current weakness is not credibility. It is time-to-understanding. A first-time visitor can tell that Somnary is serious, but they still need to infer four things:

- who the site is primarily for;
- what action they should take first;
- how a grade changes a decision;
- why this is more useful than asking a generic AI model.

The strategic answer is to make Somnary less like a static remedy encyclopedia and more like a trust-preserving decision system.

## 2. Market Context

Sleep difficulty and sleep-aid usage are large enough to support a focused site. CDC data reported that in 2024, 12.9% of US adults used some kind of sleep aid most days or every day in the prior 30 days; 5.7% used OTC medication or supplements and 3.7% used marijuana or CBD products for sleep. CDC's earlier 2020 report found 8.4% of adults used sleep medication every day or most days. That is a meaningful pool of frequent, recurring decision-makers.

Melatonin is the cleanest wedge. NIH summarized JAMA data showing US adult melatonin use rose from 0.4% in 1999-2000 to 2.1% in 2017-2018, while higher-dose use over 5 mg also rose. NCCIH says melatonin appears relatively safe short term, but long-term safety has not been established, and pediatric overdose concerns have increased. JAMA's 2023 gummy analysis found actual melatonin content ranged from 74% to 347% of the label amount, with 22 of 25 products inaccurately labeled.

This creates a clear user problem: people are using sleep aids frequently, the products are marketed with confidence, and the consumer decision environment is weak.

## 3. Recommended Positioning

Somnary should own this sentence:

> Somnary checks natural sleep remedies by human evidence, label reality, and safety boundaries before you buy or take anything.

Supporting claims:

- Evidence-graded, not popularity-ranked.
- No affiliate links and no brand money.
- Weak evidence is shown, not buried.
- Safety boundaries come before supplement enthusiasm.
- AI answers only from the reviewed Somnary corpus.

Do not position as a wellness magazine, medical provider, supplement marketplace, or sleep-coaching app. Those categories carry either weaker trust or heavier regulatory burden.

## 4. Audience Strategy

Primary audience: the evidence-seeking sleep improver. This person is tired, skeptical, and likely comparing melatonin, magnesium, CBD, valerian, L-theanine, or a blended sleep gummy. They are not necessarily anti-supplement. They are anti-being-misled.

Secondary audiences:

- cautious caregivers and parents;
- older adults and medication users;
- anxious poor sleepers;
- shift workers and travelers;
- clinicians, pharmacists, journalists, and health writers who need citation-rich summaries.

Avoid making biohackers the center of gravity. They can be useful for attention, but they reward stacks, novelty, and aggressive self-experimentation. That pressure works against Somnary's trust advantage.

## 5. IA Recommendation

The site should move from remedy-first navigation toward decision-first navigation.

Recommended top-level IA:

1. Start Here
2. Remedies
3. Goals
4. Safety
5. Label Checker
6. Evidence System
7. Community

Homepage first-screen recommendation:

- headline: “Check a sleep remedy before you take it.”
- subhead: “Human evidence, label reality, and safety boundaries for melatonin, magnesium, CBD, valerian, sleep blends, and more.”
- primary actions: “Check a remedy”, “Compare by goal”, “Read the safety guide”.
- trust strip: “No affiliate links. No brand money. Sources checked by humans.”

## 6. Content Strategy

Somnary should create content in five lanes:

1. Remedy truth pages: deep evidence reviews, already strongest on the site.
2. Decision pages: “Should I try X for Y?” pages with bottom-line guidance.
3. Safety pages: audience and risk pages, not just remedy-specific warnings.
4. Label pages: product-claim and ingredient-dose education.
5. Evidence-watch pages: updates, corrections, new trials, claim checks.

Flagship content clusters:

- Melatonin: dose, timing, long-term uncertainty, pediatric risks, gummy label accuracy.
- Sleep blends: proprietary blends, hidden doses, ingredient mismatch, label checker.
- CBD for sleep: reputation vs evidence, THC confusion, product-label issues.
- CBT-I: why the strongest evidence is not a supplement and how to find real CBT-I.
- “When to see a doctor”: sleep apnea, chronic insomnia, self-harm, restless legs, narcolepsy, REM behavior disorder.

## 7. Product Strategy

Build products that reinforce evidence discipline instead of replacing it with generic advice.

Recommended product roadmap:

- Remedy checker: search a remedy and return grade, effect, safety boundary, evidence gates.
- Label checker: paste a Supplement Facts panel and detect proprietary blends, dose mismatch, high melatonin, hidden CBD/THC risk, missing standardization, and interaction flags.
- Compare tool: compare remedies by goal, effect size, safety, evidence quality, and “who should avoid”.
- Clinician handout mode: export a clean one-page PDF for a pharmacist or GP discussion.
- Evidence change log: public changes to grades and source interpretation.
- Reader claim submission: “Send us a claim or label to check.”

Commercial products should be reader-funded, professional-facing, or tools-based before they become commerce-based. Affiliate revenue would spend the site's best trust asset.

## 8. AI Strategy

AI is useful only if it is constrained. Somnary should not launch an open-ended sleep chatbot.

The right AI product is a scoped evidence assistant that answers from Somnary's reviewed corpus, cites the exact page or source, refuses personalized diagnosis/dosing, and routes safety concerns to clinician-boundary pages. The assistant should be strongest at explaining what the evidence does and does not show.

Core AI promise:

> The assistant can summarize Somnary. It cannot outrank Somnary.

That line matters. Generic AI can produce a plausible sleep answer. Somnary's advantage is editorial control, source verification, conflict-free monetization, and explicit uncertainty.

## 9. Growth Strategy

Growth should come from claim-checking, not generic sleep tips.

The core content hook is: “We checked the claim.”

Repeatable campaign formats:

- “Does this actually help sleep?”
- “What the label says vs what the evidence says.”
- “Natural does not mean safe.”
- “The sleep supplement internet is a sales floor.”
- “The strongest sleep intervention is not a supplement.”

Best initial growth channels:

- SEO for remedy and claim queries;
- YouTube explainers and label teardowns;
- TikTok and Instagram short-form claim checks;
- X.com evidence threads and corrections log;
- newsletter as the retention engine;
- collaborations with pharmacists, sleep clinicians, skeptical health creators, and consumer-protection writers.

## 10. Regulatory And Risk Notes

Somnary is not selling supplements today, but it operates in a health-adjacent domain. Regulatory risk increases with personalization, monetization, testimonials, product ratings, and AI.

FTC guidance says health-related product claims should be truthful, not misleading, and supported by science. FDA structure/function claim rules and disease-claim boundaries matter if Somnary ever reviews, labels, or partners around specific products. FTC endorsement rules matter for influencer and creator partnerships.

Risk controls:

- Keep “educational, not medical advice” visible near decisions, not only in the footer.
- Avoid “best supplement for insomnia” unless the page explains the evidence boundary.
- Never let testimonials imply clinical efficacy.
- Disclose all collaborations, sponsorships, samples, grants, and reviewer relationships.
- Require source-backed copy for all ads.
- Require legal review before product QA scores, paid partnerships, supplement commerce, or personalized AI.

- create YouTube and short-form templates.

Days 61-90:

- prototype scoped AI assistant using reviewed corpus only;
- create clinician handout exports;
- launch collaboration pipeline;
- test paid ads against melatonin and sleep-blend landing pages;
- publish public corrections and evidence-change log.

## 12. Strategic Guardrail

Every decision should pass this test:

> Does this make Somnary more useful as an accountable evidence layer, or does it make Somnary more like the sleep-marketing internet it exists to correct?

If the answer is the second, do not ship it.
