## Research Notes Used In The Strategy

- CDC 2024: 12.9% of adults used any sleep aid most days or every day in the prior 30 days; 5.7% used OTC medications or supplements; 3.7% used marijuana or CBD products.
- CDC 2020: 8.4% of adults used sleep medication every day or most days.
- NIH/JAMA melatonin trend: adult use rose from 0.4% in 1999-2000 to 2.1% in 2017-2018; high-dose use over 5 mg rose from 2005 onward.
- NCCIH: melatonin appears relatively safe short term, but long-term safety has not been established; pediatric ingestion concerns matter.
- JAMA 2023 melatonin gummies: actual melatonin ranged from 74% to 347% of the label amount; 22 of 25 products were inaccurately labeled.
- ACP and AASM: CBT-I is first-line / strongly recommended for chronic insomnia in adults.
- FTC: health-product claims must be truthful, not misleading, and supported by science.
- FDA: dietary supplement structure/function claims have boundaries and required disclaimers; disease claims are legally different.
