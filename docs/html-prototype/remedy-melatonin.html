Grade
A

Good evidence. Well supported in human studies for sleep onset and circadian timing.

Why this grade
Best forBody-clock timing, jet lag, shift work, delayed sleep phase.
Not forKnocking yourself out or fixing chronic insomnia alone.
Biggest riskMorning drowsiness and pediatric misuse.
Studied doseUsually 0.3-2 mg, 30-60 minutes before bed.

Evidence in one sentence

Consistent, moderate evidence that melatonin helps you fall asleep faster when timing matches your body clock.

What changes your decision

Your dose, timing, age, medications, and the quality of the product you choose.

Safety boundary

Short-term use for most adults. Avoid in pregnancy without medical advice and use caution in children.
