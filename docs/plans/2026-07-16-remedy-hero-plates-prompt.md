**Somnary — remedy hero plates: 31 full-bleed linocut hero images**

You are generating a family of **full-bleed hero images** for the remedy pages
of Somnary, an evidence-graded reference for natural sleep remedies. The site's
illustration language is **hand-carved linocut**: bold silhouettes, slightly
irregular edges, visible gouge marks, chunky lines. These heroes extend that
language into a richer register — **earthy, textured, atmospheric, almost
romantic** — like large-format woodblock prints of each plant's world at dusk.

**One grammar, 31 moods.** Every image must read as the same printmaker's hand:

- Linocut/woodcut carving vocabulary throughout — carved marks, ink texture,
  paper grain, slight misregistration between color layers. No photorealism, no
  airbrush, no digital gradients (tonal transitions come from carved hatching
  and layered ink, like a reduction print). Texture density matches the
  approved melatonin plate — its grain is the family standard; don't get finer.
- **3–5 ink colors + paper tone per image**, muted and earthy. Each remedy gets
  its OWN palette (below) — that per-remedy palette is the point: each
  supplement has a unique vibe.
- Mood: dusk, night, moonlight, lamplight — the hour of sleep. Romantic,
  hushed, botanical.
- Landscape composition **2880×1620 (16:9), PNG, sRGB**. Carving stays chunky
  enough to survive a 50% crop (the site crops heroes responsively).
- **Composition rule:** rich detail lives in the upper two-thirds and edges;
  keep the **lower third calm and tonally even** (a quiet band of sky, water,
  ground, or paper) — the page overlays the title, grade, and a masked icon
  emblem there. The focal subject stays **within the central 60% of the
  width** (mobile crops). **Do not render any icon, emblem, badge, or empty
  circle placeholder into the image.**

**Hard constraints (non-negotiable):**

- **No text, letters, numbers, or typography** of any kind in the image.
- **No people** (silhouettes included), no branded or generic **product
  shots** — no pills, capsules, bottles, jars, gummies, packaging. Depict the
  plant, mineral, or idea itself. (Traditional objects are fine where noted,
  e.g. a kava bowl, a teacup.)
- **Avoid vermilion/scarlet `#E34234` as a dominant hue** — that color is
  reserved for safety warnings on the site. Reds should be deep garnets,
  lacquers, rusts.
- The brand oxblood `#7E1F2B` may appear as an ink but must not dominate —
  these pages should each feel like their own world, not the brand color
  repainted.
- Palettes are botanical, never evaluative — do not make any remedy look
  deliberately dull or sickly; the site's evidence grades are communicated
  separately.

**Process:** generate **melatonin first** as the north star; hold the family to
its texture/mood standard. After it's approved, batch the rest and deliver a
contact sheet. Name files `{slug}.png` exactly as below.
